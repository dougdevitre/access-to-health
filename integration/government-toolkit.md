# Government Toolkit — Legislation, Intergovernmental, and Constituent Services

> For elected officials, legislative staff, city/county managers, and intergovernmental coordinators
> How government at every level can use public health data and systems

## For Elected Officials

### Why You Need This

Your constituents' health is shaped more by decisions made in city hall, the statehouse, and Congress than by decisions made in a doctor's office. Housing, transportation, food access, environmental quality, economic opportunity, and public safety are all health issues — and they're all within government's sphere of influence.

**The ask**: Use health data in your decisions. Fund the infrastructure that prevents disease. Partner with the health department — they're your experts.

### Health Data for Your District

Request from your health department:

| Data | What It Tells You | How to Use It |
|------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Life expectancy by ZIP/tract** | Where your constituents live longest and shortest | Target investment in lowest life-expectancy areas |
| **Leading causes of death** | What kills your constituents | Prioritize prevention (policy, funding, programs) |
| **Uninsured rate** | Who lacks healthcare access | Support enrollment assistance, FQHC funding |
| **Infant mortality by race** | Birth equity in your district | Fund doula programs, home visiting, prenatal access |
| **Overdose deaths** | Substance use crisis severity | Fund harm reduction, treatment access, naloxone |
| **Food insecurity rate** | Who's going hungry | Support SNAP outreach, food pantries, school meals |
| **Lead testing results by ZIP** | Where children are being poisoned | Fund lead remediation, enforce housing codes |
| **Mental health ED visits** | 988/crisis system demand | Fund crisis services, school mental health |

**How to request**: Contact the health director. Data requests typically take 2-4 weeks for de-identified aggregate data. See `scripts/team-sops.md` SOP 6.

## Legislative Toolkit

### Model Legislation Categories

| Category | Examples | Health Impact |
|----------|---------|---------------|
| **Tobacco** | Tobacco 21, smoke-free workplaces, flavored tobacco ban | Reduces youth initiation, secondhand smoke exposure |
| **Nutrition** | Sugar-sweetened beverage tax, SNAP incentives, school nutrition | Reduces obesity, diabetes, food insecurity |
| **Housing** | Healthy housing codes, lead inspection, eviction prevention | Reduces lead poisoning, respiratory disease, homelessness |
| **Transportation** | Complete Streets, Vision Zero, transit funding | Reduces injuries, improves access, increases activity |
| **Substance use** | Naloxone access, Good Samaritan, syringe services, MAT in jails | Reduces overdose deaths, HIV/HCV, recidivism |
| **Mental health** | 988 funding, school counselors, crisis services, parity enforcement | Reduces suicide, ED boarding, untreated mental illness |
| **Firearms** | Extreme risk protection orders, child access prevention, safe storage | Reduces suicide, homicide, unintentional injury |
| **Environment** | Environmental justice review, buffer zones, pollution standards | Reduces asthma, cancer, environmental racism |
| **Workforce** | Paid sick leave, minimum wage, occupational safety | Reduces presenteeism, poverty, workplace injury |
| **Insurance** | Medicaid expansion, postpartum extension, dental coverage | Increases access, reduces uncompensated care |

### How to Use Health Department Testimony

Health departments can:
- Provide expert testimony at hearings (non-partisan, data-driven)
- Prepare fiscal notes (health cost of inaction)
- Draft bill language for health provisions
- Analyze proposed legislation for health impact
- Provide constituent-level data for advocacy

**See**: `features/advocacy-toolkit.md` for testimony templates and legislator visit guides.

### Legislative Session Calendar Integration

```
PRE-SESSION (2 months before)
├── Health department briefs legislators on top 3-5 priorities
├── Prepare fact sheets with district-level data
├── Draft model bill language
└── Build coalition support

DURING SESSION
├── Track health-related bills weekly
├── Provide testimony at committee hearings
├── Issue action alerts to advocacy network
├── Monitor budget hearings — submit written testimony
└── Brief elected officials before key votes

POST-SESSION
├── Summary of health legislation outcomes
├── Implementation planning for passed bills
├── Thank supportive legislators
└── Document lessons for next session
```

See `communication/internal-playbook.md` Section 2.4 for the full legislative communication calendar.

## Intergovernmental Coordination

### Health in All Policies (HiAP) Framework

**Principle**: Every government decision should consider health impacts. Health departments should be consulted on non-health decisions that affect health.

| Government Function | Health Connection | Health Department Input |
|---------------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Planning & Zoning** | Built environment → activity, food access, pollution | HIA for major decisions. Health data for comp plans |
| **Housing** | Housing quality → lead, asthma, mental health | Healthy housing standards. Lead data. SDOH screening |
| **Transportation** | Mobility → healthcare access, injuries, air quality | Pedestrian injury data. Transit equity analysis |
| **Parks & Recreation** | Green space → activity, mental health, social cohesion | Community health data. Co-locate health services |
| **Economic Development** | Employment → income, insurance, stress | HIA for major developments. Health workforce needs |
| **Public Safety** | Violence → injury, trauma, community health | Violence data. Trauma-informed approaches. Co-responder |
| **Education** | Schools → child health, mental health, nutrition | School health data. Coordinated school health |
| **Water/Sewer** | Infrastructure → water quality, environmental health | Water quality monitoring. Fluoridation. Lead service lines |
| **Emergency Management** | Disasters → injury, displacement, mental health | Public health emergency planning. Medical countermeasures |

### Standing Intergovernmental Meetings

Recommend establishing:
1. **Monthly**: Health Director + City/County Manager check-in
2. **Quarterly**: Cross-department health equity meeting (health, housing, planning, transportation, parks, police)
3. **Annually**: Joint health-planning data sharing workshop
4. **As needed**: HIA review for major projects

## Funding and Budget Advocacy

### Making the Case for Public Health Funding

**For budget hearings**:
- Lead with constituent impact: "[#] people in your district were served last year"
- Show ROI: "Every $1 we spend on [program] saves $[X] in [healthcare/productivity]"
- Show consequences: "Without funding, we will lose [specific services]"
- Be specific: "We are requesting $[amount] for [specific use] serving [specific population]"
- Bring community voice: Partner or client testimony (with consent)

**For appropriations**:
- Align with legislator's priorities and committee assignments
- Provide district-level data (not just statewide)
- Show federal match: "Your $[state amount] unlocks $[federal amount]"
- Compare to peer states: "We rank [#] out of 50 in per-capita public health spending"

## Public Health Authority Reference

### What the Health Department Can Do (Legal Authority)

| Authority | Scope | Relevance |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| **Disease investigation** | Investigate and control communicable disease | Outbreak response, contact tracing |
| **Isolation and quarantine** | Restrict movement of infectious individuals | Emergency situations with due process |
| **Inspection and enforcement** | Inspect food establishments, pools, housing | Food safety, environmental health |
| **Emergency orders** | Issue public health emergency orders | Closures, advisories, evacuations |
| **Data collection** | Collect health data for surveillance | Reportable diseases, vital statistics |
| **Regulation** | Adopt and enforce health regulations | Per local board of health authority |
| **Licensure** | Issue and revoke health-related permits | Food permits, body art, etc. |

**Key principle**: Public health authority exists to protect the public, not to punish. Exercise with minimum restriction necessary and full due process.

See `references/missouri-public-health.md` for Missouri-specific legal citations.
