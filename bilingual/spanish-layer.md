# Capa Bilingüe / Spanish Language Layer

> Bilingual support for public health outreach, screening, and navigation
> Not a translation — a culturally adapted communication framework

---

## Language Access Standards

### When to Use Professional Interpretation
- **Always** for clinical encounters, screening, consent, and sensitive conversations
- **Never** use family members (especially children) as interpreters for medical or SDOH content
- **Never** rely on Google Translate for health content
- Offer language preference at first contact: "¿Prefiere hablar en español? / Do you prefer to speak in Spanish?"

### Interpreter Modes
| Mode | Use When | Standard |
|------|----------|----------|
| In-person interpreter | Complex clinical, mental health, sensitive topics | Preferred |
| Video Remote Interpreting (VRI) | In-person not available, visual cues needed | HIPAA-compliant platform required |
| Phone interpretation | Scheduling, brief questions, follow-up | Language line services |
| Bilingual staff | Certified bilingual, routine interactions | Must be formally assessed (not self-reported) |

## SDOH Screening Scripts (Spanish)

### Quick Screen — 5 Preguntas

| # | English | Español |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | In the past year, have you or your family ever had to go without food, heat, or a place to sleep? | En el último año, ¿usted o su familia han tenido que pasar sin comida, calefacción o un lugar donde dormir? |
| 2 | Do you have trouble getting to medical appointments or the pharmacy? | ¿Tiene dificultad para llegar a sus citas médicas o a la farmacia? |
| 3 | In the past year, have you been unable to get a medication or treatment that a doctor prescribed? | En el último año, ¿no ha podido obtener un medicamento o tratamiento que un doctor le recetó? |
| 4 | Do you feel safe where you live? | ¿Se siente seguro/a donde vive? |
| 5 | In the past 2 weeks, have you felt down, depressed, or hopeless? | En las últimas 2 semanas, ¿se ha sentido desanimado/a, deprimido/a o sin esperanza? |

### Screening Introduction Script

**English**: "We ask these questions to everyone because your health is affected by many things — not just medical care. Your answers help us connect you with resources in the community. Everything you share is confidential."

**Español**: "Le hacemos estas preguntas a todas las personas porque su salud es afectada por muchas cosas — no solo por la atención médica. Sus respuestas nos ayudan a conectarle con recursos en la comunidad. Todo lo que comparta es confidencial."

## Cultural Concepts in Latino/Hispanic Health

### Familismo
- Family is the primary support unit — decisions are often collective, not individual
- Include family in health conversations when the client invites it
- Respect parental and elder authority
- "Family" includes extended family, godparents (padrinos/madrinas), and close friends

### Personalismo
- Warm, personal relationships are valued over institutional efficiency
- Take time for small talk before business (¿Cómo está? ¿Cómo está su familia?)
- Use formal address (usted) until invited to use informal (tú)
- Handshakes, eye contact, and warmth build trust

### Respeto
- Deep respect for elders, authority figures, and professionals
- Clients may agree outwardly but disagree privately — check understanding with teach-back
- Never dismiss folk beliefs or home remedies — integrate them respectfully

### Confianza (Trust)
- Trust is built through consistent, respectful, personal relationships — not institutions
- Promotoras (community health workers from the community) are the gold standard for outreach
- Distrust of government systems is rational — immigration enforcement, public charge rule, and deportation fears are real barriers
- **Always clarify**: "We do not share information with immigration authorities"

### Machismo / Marianismo
- Traditional gender roles may influence who makes health decisions, who seeks care, and who is comfortable discussing sensitive topics
- Do NOT assume — ask. Many families are non-traditional
- Men may delay seeking care due to cultural expectations of strength
- Women may prioritize family health over their own

### Susto, Nervios, and Folk Illness
- **Susto** (fright): Believed to cause soul loss from a traumatic event. Symptoms overlap with PTSD/anxiety
- **Nervios** (nerves): Generalized distress, anxiety, depression. Culturally acceptable way to express mental health symptoms
- **Empacho** (blocked digestion): GI complaints attributed to food blockage
- **Mal de ojo** (evil eye): Illness caused by envy or strong gaze, especially in children
- **Do NOT dismiss**: Acknowledge, ask about traditional remedies, integrate with medical care. "I respect that. Let's also make sure we check [medical concern] to keep you safe."

## Promotora / Community Health Worker Model

### What Is a Promotora?
A **promotora de salud** is a community health worker who is a trusted member of the community they serve. They share language, culture, lived experience, and social networks with the population.

### Promotora Scope
| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Outreach | Door-to-door, community events, churches, tiendas, laundromats, soccer fields |
| Screening | Administer SDOH screening, connect to resources |
| Navigation | Accompany clients to appointments, translate, advocate |
| Education | Lead charlas (health talks) in familiar settings |
| Enrollment | Assist with Medicaid, WIC, SNAP, marketplace applications |
| Follow-up | Check on referral completion, identify new needs |
| Trust-building | Serve as bridge between community and health system |

### Charla (Health Talk) Format
1. Welcome and introductions (5 min)
2. Opening question: "¿Qué saben ustedes sobre [topic]?" (What do you know about [topic]?) (5 min)
3. Key information — 3 messages max, plain language, visual aids (15 min)
4. Activity or demonstration (10 min)
5. Questions and discussion (10 min)
6. Resources and next steps (5 min)
7. Evaluation — verbal or simple written form (bilingual)

### Charla Topics (Ready-to-Use)
- Diabetes prevention and management (La diabetes: lo que necesita saber)
- Healthy eating on a budget (Comer saludable sin gastar mucho)
- Prenatal care (Cuidado durante el embarazo)
- Childhood immunizations (Vacunas para sus hijos)
- Mental health and stress (La salud mental importa)
- Domestic violence resources (Si necesita ayuda, no está sola)
- Know your rights: healthcare access regardless of status (Sus derechos de salud)

## Social Media Content (Bilingual Templates)

### Template 1: Health Event Promotion
**English**: Free [service] this [day]! No insurance needed. No ID required. [Location], [time]. Questions? Call [number].

**Español**: ¡[Servicio] gratis este [día]! No necesita seguro médico. No se requiere identificación. [Ubicación], [hora]. ¿Preguntas? Llame al [número].

### Template 2: Resource Awareness
**English**: Did you know? [Resource] provides free [service] for families in [area]. Call [number] or visit [location]. Available in English and Spanish.

**Español**: ¿Sabía que [recurso] ofrece [servicio] gratis para familias en [área]? Llame al [número] o visite [ubicación]. Disponible en inglés y español.

### Template 3: Health Tip
**English**: Your health matters! [One actionable health tip]. Talk to your doctor or call [number] for more information.

**Español**: ¡Su salud importa! [Consejo de salud]. Hable con su doctor o llame al [número] para más información.

### Template 4: Crisis Resource
**English**: If you or someone you know needs help: [Resource name] [Phone number] — available 24/7, in Spanish. You are not alone.

**Español**: Si usted o alguien que conoce necesita ayuda: [Nombre del recurso] [Teléfono] — disponible 24/7, en español. No está solo/a.

## Immigration-Sensitive Service Delivery

### Public Charge Rule Guidance
- As of current guidance, using the following programs does NOT affect immigration status:
  - Medicaid (emergency or for children/pregnant persons)
  - SNAP
  - WIC
  - CHIP
  - Public health services (immunizations, communicable disease treatment)
  - Emergency shelter
  - Head Start
- **Always check current policy** — rules change with administrations
- Provide information, not legal advice. Refer to immigration legal services for individual guidance

### Safe Spaces Protocol
- Health department services are available regardless of immigration status
- Staff will NOT ask about immigration status unless required by a specific program
- Staff will NOT share client information with immigration enforcement (ICE/CBP)
- Post visible signage in English and Spanish: "This is a safe space. We do not ask about immigration status."
- If immigration enforcement arrives at a health department facility, contact legal counsel immediately. Do NOT provide client records without a valid judicial warrant (not an ICE administrative warrant)
