# Email Sequences — 8 Lifecycle Sequences (30 Total Emails)

> Pre-built email sequences for public health engagement
> Each sequence includes: trigger, frequency, subject lines, and content structure

---

## Sequence 1: New Client Welcome (4 emails)

**Trigger**: Client completes first visit or enrollment
**Frequency**: Day 1, Day 3, Day 7, Day 14

**Email 1 (Day 1): Welcome**
Subject: Welcome to [Health Department] — here's what to know
- Thank them for choosing your services
- What to expect (hours, contact info, how to schedule)
- One resource they can use right now
- Contact info for their assigned CHW/navigator

**Email 2 (Day 3): Resources**
Subject: Free resources available to you and your family
- 3-4 most-used community resources with phone numbers
- "These services are free and confidential"
- Highlight: SDOH resources (food, housing, transportation)

**Email 3 (Day 7): Your Health Team**
Subject: Meet your health team
- Introduce their care team (provider, CHW, navigator)
- How to reach each person
- Remind of upcoming appointments

**Email 4 (Day 14): Check-In**
Subject: How are things going?
- Brief check-in
- Ask if they were able to connect with any referrals
- Satisfaction survey link (3 questions max)
- Reminder of available services

## Sequence 2: Prenatal/Postpartum (6 emails)

**Trigger**: Pregnancy confirmation or prenatal enrollment
**Frequency**: Monthly through pregnancy, then Week 1 and Month 1 postpartum

**Email 1 (First Trimester): Getting Started**
Subject: Your healthy pregnancy journey starts here
- Prenatal visit schedule
- WIC enrollment information
- Folic acid, nutrition basics
- What to ask your provider

**Email 2 (Second Trimester): Growing Strong**
Subject: You're halfway there — here's what's next
- Anatomy scan and glucose screening reminders
- Childbirth education classes
- Doula program information
- Exercise and nutrition during pregnancy

**Email 3 (Third Trimester): Getting Ready**
Subject: Getting ready for baby
- Birth plan considerations
- Hospital bag checklist
- Car seat safety
- Breastfeeding resources and support

**Email 4 (36+ Weeks): Almost Time**
Subject: Your checklist for the final weeks
- When to go to the hospital (signs of labor)
- Postpartum warning signs to know NOW (before delivery)
- Pediatrician selection
- Postpartum support resources

**Email 5 (Week 1 Postpartum): Congratulations**
Subject: Congratulations! Here's support for you and baby
- Newborn care basics
- Breastfeeding support (lactation consultant, peer counselor)
- Postpartum depression screening — "It's normal to feel overwhelmed"
- When to call the doctor (for you and baby)

**Email 6 (Month 1 Postpartum): Checking In On YOU**
Subject: How are YOU doing?
- Postpartum mood check-in (normalize struggle)
- Postpartum visit reminder — "This appointment is for YOUR health"
- Family planning resources
- Community support groups

## Sequence 3: Immunization Reminder (3 emails)

**Trigger**: Child due for vaccines per schedule
**Frequency**: 2 weeks before, 3 days before, 1 day after missed appointment

**Email 1 (2 Weeks Before): Upcoming Vaccines**
Subject: [Child's name]'s vaccines are due this month
- Which vaccines are due and what they protect against (plain language)
- Where to get them (clinic locations, hours)
- "Free — no insurance needed"
- Schedule appointment link/phone

**Email 2 (3 Days Before): Reminder**
Subject: Reminder: [Child's name]'s vaccine appointment
- Date, time, location
- What to bring
- "You can ask questions — we're here to help"

**Email 3 (After Missed Appointment): Reschedule**
Subject: We missed you! Let's reschedule [Child's name]'s vaccines
- No judgment — life happens
- Why it's important to catch up
- Easy reschedule options
- Walk-in availability

## Sequence 4: Chronic Disease Self-Management (4 emails)

**Trigger**: Diagnosis or enrollment in chronic disease program
**Frequency**: Week 1, Week 2, Month 1, Month 3

**Email 1 (Week 1): You're Not Alone**
Subject: Living with [condition] — you've got support
- Acknowledgment: A new diagnosis can feel overwhelming
- What the condition means (plain language, not scary)
- Your care team and how to reach them
- One thing you can do today

**Email 2 (Week 2): Small Steps**
Subject: Small steps make a big difference
- One evidence-based self-management tip
- How to track your progress (app, journal, log)
- Community support group information
- Recipe or activity suggestion

**Email 3 (Month 1): Progress Check**
Subject: One month in — how are you doing?
- Celebrate any progress (no matter how small)
- Common challenges at this stage and how to handle them
- Medication adherence reminder
- Upcoming appointment reminder

**Email 4 (Month 3): Staying On Track**
Subject: Keeping your health on track
- Longer-term management strategies
- Screening and monitoring schedule
- Community resources (exercise programs, nutrition classes)
- Invite to peer support group

## Sequence 5: Community Event Follow-Up (3 emails)

**Trigger**: Attended community health event or screening
**Frequency**: Day 1, Week 1, Month 1

**Email 1 (Day 1): Thank You**
Subject: Thanks for joining us at [event name]!
- Thank them for attending
- Summary of what was offered
- If they were screened: "Your results are [status]. Here's what that means."
- Resources discussed at the event

**Email 2 (Week 1): Follow-Up Resources**
Subject: Resources from [event name]
- Links/contacts for all resources shared at the event
- If referral was made: "Have you been able to connect with [resource]?"
- Upcoming events

**Email 3 (Month 1): Stay Connected**
Subject: Stay connected with [Health Department]
- Newsletter signup
- Advisory board opportunity
- Upcoming events
- Social media links
- "Your voice matters" — survey link

## Sequence 6: Provider Communication (3 emails)

**Trigger**: Clinical advisory, guideline change, or outbreak
**Frequency**: As needed (not more than biweekly for routine)

**Email 1: Clinical Advisory**
Subject: CLINICAL ADVISORY: [Condition] — [Action Required]
- What's happening (1-2 sentences)
- Clinical guidance (testing, treatment, reporting)
- Reporting requirements and how to report
- Resources and contacts

**Email 2: Guideline Update**
Subject: Updated guidance: [Topic]
- What changed and why
- New recommendations (table format)
- Implementation timeline
- Training/webinar link
- FAQ document link

**Email 3: Data Update for Providers**
Subject: Local data update: [Topic]
- Key statistics for their service area
- Comparison to state/national
- What this means for clinical practice
- Resources for patients

## Sequence 7: Advocacy/Action Alert (3 emails)

**Trigger**: Legislative action, comment period, or policy decision
**Frequency**: Alert, reminder, outcome

**Email 1: Action Alert**
Subject: ACTION NEEDED: [Issue] — [Deadline]
- What's happening and why it matters to health
- Specific action (call, email, attend hearing, submit comment)
- Talking points (3 bullets)
- Deadline
- Links and contacts

**Email 2: Reminder**
Subject: REMINDER: [Deadline] to [action] on [issue]
- Quick recap
- "Have you taken action yet?"
- Updated talking points if situation has changed
- Easy action link

**Email 3: Outcome**
Subject: UPDATE: What happened with [issue]
- Result (passed, failed, amended, tabled)
- What this means for community health
- Thank supporters
- Next steps

## Sequence 8: Re-Engagement (4 emails)

**Trigger**: 90 days of inactivity (no visits, no email opens)
**Frequency**: Day 90, Day 100, Day 110, Day 120 (final)

**Email 1 (Day 90): We Miss You**
Subject: We haven't seen you in a while — everything OK?
- Warm, concerned tone
- Reminder of available services
- "If your needs have changed, we're here to help"
- Schedule an appointment link

**Email 2 (Day 100): What's New**
Subject: New at [Health Department] — we think you'll want to know
- 2-3 new services, programs, or events
- "We're always growing to serve you better"

**Email 3 (Day 110): Direct Ask**
Subject: Can we help with anything?
- "Is there a barrier keeping you from coming in? We want to fix it."
- Transportation? Hours? Childcare? Language? Cost?
- Contact information for navigator

**Email 4 (Day 120): Final Check**
Subject: Should we keep in touch?
- "We want to respect your inbox"
- Option to stay subscribed, reduce frequency, or unsubscribe
- "If you ever need us, we're here: [phone, website, address]"
- Unsubscribe if no response (CAN-SPAM compliance)
