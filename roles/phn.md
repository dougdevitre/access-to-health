# Role: Public Health Nurse (PHN)

> Pod: Clinical | Key partnerships: CHW, MCH, SHC, EPI, BHC, DIS
> Clinical expertise at the population level — prevention, protection, and promotion

---

## Role Summary

Public health nurses apply nursing science and public health principles to promote health, prevent disease, and protect populations. Unlike clinical nurses in hospital settings, PHNs work at the community level — in schools, homes, clinics, and the field.

**Core competencies**: Clinical assessment, immunization administration, communicable disease follow-up, maternal/child health, chronic disease management, population health, case management, health education

**Licensure**: Registered Nurse (RN) with public health nursing experience. BSN preferred. Some states require specific PH nursing certification.

---

## 5 Core Workflows

### Workflow 1: Immunization Services

**Trigger**: Client presents for vaccination, school compliance, outbreak response, or catch-up

**Routine immunization clinic**:
1. Review immunization history (state registry + client records)
2. Assess using ACIP schedule (age-appropriate vaccines due)
3. Screen for contraindications and precautions
4. Provide Vaccine Information Statements (VIS) — required by law
5. Obtain informed consent
6. Administer vaccine(s) per established protocols
7. Document in client record AND state immunization registry
8. Schedule next appointment
9. Monitor for adverse events (15-30 min observation for anaphylaxis risk)
10. Report adverse events to VAERS if applicable

**Catch-up scheduling**: Use minimum interval table (CDC catch-up schedule). Prioritize vaccines based on outbreak risk and school entry requirements.

**Vaccine hesitancy response**: Use HEAR framework (`messaging/trust-rebuilding-playbook.md`). Listen, empathize, provide evidence, recommend next step. Respect autonomy. Never shame.

**Mass vaccination / outbreak response**: VFC vaccine ordering, cold chain management, clinic setup, volunteer coordination, documentation, data entry.

**Key artifacts**: Immunization assessment, compliance reports, VFC inventory, school coverage rates

### Workflow 2: Maternal and Child Health

**Trigger**: Prenatal enrollment, newborn referral, high-risk maternal follow-up

**Prenatal home visits** (coordinate with MCH and home visiting program):
- Health assessment at enrollment
- Screen for depression (PHQ-9), IPV, SUD, SDOH at every visit
- Nutrition counseling and WIC referral
- Childbirth education
- Birth plan support
- Breastfeeding preparation
- Postpartum warning sign education (teach BEFORE delivery)

**Newborn follow-up**:
- Newborn screening result follow-up (metabolic, hearing, CCHD)
- Breastfeeding support and assessment
- Immunization schedule initiation (Hepatitis B at birth)
- Safe sleep education (ABCs: Alone, Back, Crib)
- Postpartum depression screening for mother
- Car seat safety
- Connect to pediatric medical home

**High-risk follow-up**: Preterm infants, substance-exposed newborns, NAS, NICU graduates, teens, mothers with chronic conditions, positive toxicology screens

**Key partnerships**: MCH (program coordination), NUT (WIC/breastfeeding), BHC (depression/SUD), CHW (navigation)

### Workflow 3: Communicable Disease Follow-Up

**Trigger**: Reportable disease case assigned by EPI

**Standard case follow-up**:
1. Receive case assignment from EPI
2. Contact patient (phone or field visit)
3. Complete case investigation form (demographics, symptoms, onset, exposures, risk factors)
4. Provide disease-specific education (transmission, treatment, prevention)
5. Ensure treatment completion (especially STIs, TB, HepC)
6. Identify contacts for DIS follow-up if applicable
7. Specimen collection if needed (blood draw, swabs, stool)
8. Report findings to EPI for surveillance

**TB-specific**:
- Directly Observed Therapy (DOT): Observe patient taking each dose
- Monitor for side effects (hepatotoxicity, visual changes, rash)
- Contact investigation coordination with DIS
- Treatment duration: 6-9 months (standard), longer for MDR-TB
- Isolation guidance until non-infectious

**STI-specific**:
- Expedited Partner Therapy (EPT) per state law
- Coordinate with DIS for partner notification
- Linkage to HIV testing and PrEP
- Confidentiality per state STI laws

**Key artifacts**: Case investigation forms, DOT logs, specimen collection logs, treatment completion records

### Workflow 4: Chronic Disease Screening and Management

**Trigger**: Community screening event, clinical encounter, referral from CHW or provider

**Screenings administered**:
| Screening | Population | Protocol |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Blood pressure | Adults ≥18, every encounter | JNC guidelines; refer ≥130/80 |
| Blood glucose / A1c | Adults at risk (obesity, family history, high-risk populations) | ADA guidelines |
| BMI | All ages | Calculate and counsel |
| Lead (BLL) | Children 1-2 (universal), 3-6 (risk-based) | Capillary → venous confirm |
| Depression (PHQ-9) | Adults at clinical encounter | Score ≥10 → BHC referral |
| Developmental (ASQ) | Children at 9, 18, 24, 30 months | Refer if below cutoff |
| Vision / Hearing | School-age per schedule | Refer failures to specialist |

**Chronic disease management support**:
- Hypertension: Home BP monitoring education, medication adherence, DASH diet
- Diabetes: A1c monitoring, foot exam reminders, DPP referral for prediabetes
- Asthma: Trigger identification, action plan, spacer technique
- Heart failure: Daily weight monitoring, symptom recognition, sodium restriction

**Population approach**: Target screenings in communities with highest burden. Mobile clinics, community events, barbershop/salon partnerships, church health ministries.

---

### Workflow 5: School Health

**Trigger**: School year operations, mandated screenings, communicable disease events

**Mandated screenings** (coordinate with SHC):
- Vision: K, 1, 3, 5, 7
- Hearing: K, 1, 3, 5, 7
- Scoliosis: Girls 5-6, Boys 7-8 (varies by state)
- Dental: Varies by jurisdiction
- BMI: Per district policy

**School nursing responsibilities**:
- Administer medications per physician order and parent consent
- Manage Individualized Health Plans (IHP) for chronic conditions
- Emergency care: Epinephrine, glucagon, seizure rescue medications
- Immunization compliance review (`scripts/team-sops.md` SOP 8)
- Communicable disease surveillance and exclusion/re-admission
- Health education integration
- Staff training: Diabetes care, epinephrine use, seizure first aid, asthma

**Key partnerships**: SHC (school coordination), HED (health education), BHC (mental health), NUT (nutrition)
