# Role: Behavioral Health Clinician (BHC)

> Pod: Behavioral Health | Key partnerships: SUP, CHW, PHN, SHC, EPI
> Licensed mental health professional providing assessment, treatment, and crisis services

## Workflows
### 1: Behavioral Health Assessment
Screen using validated tools (PHQ-9, GAD-7, AUDIT-C, PC-PTSD-5, C-SSRS). Safety assessment at every encounter (suicidal ideation, homicidal ideation, access to lethal means). Comprehensive biopsychosocial assessment. Diagnosis using DSM-5 criteria. Treatment planning (therapy modality, frequency, referrals). Coordinate with PCP for integrated care. **Key**: Culturally responsive assessment — misdiagnosis rates are higher for Black and Latino clients. See `populations/POPULATION-REGISTRY.md`.

### 2: Crisis Intervention
988 integration. Mobile crisis response. Safety planning (Stanley-Brown model). Lethal means counseling. Involuntary evaluation when imminent danger exists (follow state law and due process). De-escalation. Warm handoff to ongoing care. **Never**: Leave a person in acute crisis without a safety plan and follow-up contact within 24 hours.

### 3: Integrated Behavioral Health
Embedded in primary care or public health settings. Brief interventions (15-30 min). Same-day access. Warm handoff from PHN or CHW. Treat depression, anxiety, trauma, substance use, grief, adjustment disorders. Consult with medical providers. Population-level screening programs.

### 4: Group Therapy and Psychoeducation
Evidence-based groups: CBT for depression, DBT skills, trauma processing (CPT/PE), grief support, parenting (Triple P, Incredible Years), anger management. Coordinate with HED for health education components. Ensure cultural adaptation. Bilingual groups when possible.

**Artifact**: Behavioral health assessment, safety plan, treatment plan, group curriculum, crisis contact log. Template: `artifacts/role-artifacts.md` #5.

# Role: Environmental Health Specialist (EHS)

> Pod: Environment | Key partnerships: EPI, PHN, POL, CES, HCS
> Protecting communities from environmental hazards through inspection, investigation, and enforcement

## Workflows
### 1: Food Safety Inspection
Routine inspections per jurisdiction schedule (typically 1-4x/year by risk level). Assess: food temperature control, employee hygiene, cross-contamination prevention, pest control, facility maintenance, water/sewage. Issue violations (critical vs. non-critical). Enforce corrective actions with timelines. Re-inspect. Closure authority for imminent health hazards. Report foodborne illness complaints to EPI.

### 2: Environmental Hazard Investigation
Respond to community complaints (odors, contamination, illegal dumping). Air, water, and soil sampling. Facility permit review. EJScreen and historical contamination research. Collaborate with EPA, state DEQ, and local government. Community notification of findings (plain language). See `workflows/cross-role-workflows-expanded.md` Workflow 5.

### 3: Lead Hazard Assessment
Respond to elevated blood lead levels (referral from PHN). Home inspection for lead paint, dust, and soil. Water testing. Identify and document lead hazards. Issue lead hazard orders to property owners. Monitor remediation. Coordinate with housing authority. Tenant rights education. See `messaging/campaign-builder.md` Campaign 6.

### 4: Water and Pool Safety
Public pool and spa inspections (chemistry, safety equipment, barriers, signage). Private well water testing. Public water system monitoring support. Recreational water advisories (beaches, lakes). Respond to boil water advisories. Water fluoridation monitoring.

**Artifact**: Inspection report, violation notice, environmental assessment, sampling results, lead hazard order. Template: `artifacts/role-artifacts.md` #4.

# Role: Maternal-Child Health Specialist (MCH)

> Pod: Maternal-Child Health | Key partnerships: PHN, NUT, BHC, CHW, CES
> Programs and systems for healthy pregnancies, births, and early childhood

## Workflows
### 1: MCH Program Management
Manage Title V block grant activities. Oversee home visiting programs (NFP, PAT, HFA). Coordinate with WIC, Medicaid, FQHCs. Track National Outcome Measures (NOMs) and National Performance Measures (NPMs). Submit annual Title V application and report.

### 2: Maternal Mortality and Morbidity Review
Coordinate MMRC (Maternal Mortality Review Committee). Case identification, medical record abstraction, de-identified case presentation. Committee review: Was it pregnancy-related? Was it preventable? What were contributing factors? Document recommendations. Track implementation. Publish aggregate annual report. See `workflows/cross-role-workflows-expanded.md` Workflow 3.

### 3: Perinatal Systems Coordination
Coordinate perinatal regionalization (ensuring high-risk pregnancies deliver at appropriate level hospitals). Facilitate obstetric emergency drills. Support birthing facility quality improvement (hemorrhage and hypertension bundles). Doula program development and referral. Community-based prenatal/postpartum support.

### 4: Early Childhood Systems
Developmental screening coordination (ASQ). Early intervention referral (Part C). Help Me Grow system linkage. Lead screening follow-up. Infant safe sleep education and SIDS prevention. Child fatality review participation.

**Artifact**: Title V application/report, MMRC annual report, home visiting program data, perinatal quality metrics.

# Role: Health Communications Specialist (HCS)

> Pod: Operations | Key partnerships: HDO, EPI, HED, CES, all roles
> Every word that leaves the health department — press, social, web, print — flows through HCS

## Workflows
### 1: Media Relations
Media inquiry response (SOP 3 in `scripts/team-sops.md`). Interview preparation. Press release drafting. Media monitoring. Reporter relationship management. Op-ed development. See `communication/external-playbook.md` Section 1.

### 2: Crisis Communication
First 30 minutes: Pause scheduled posts, draft holding statement. First 2 hours: Substantive update. Ongoing: Regular updates at defined intervals. Manage social media during crisis (no arguing, no deleting unless threats/HIPAA). Coordinate messaging with partners. After-action review. See `communication/external-playbook.md` Section 2.2.

### 3: Campaign Development
Audience research. Message development and testing. Creative production (graphics, video, print). Channel strategy. Launch coordination. Performance monitoring. See `messaging/campaign-builder.md` for 6 ready-to-deploy campaigns and `messaging/social-media-library.md` for 27 posts.

### 4: Digital and Content Management
Website content management (WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility). Social media strategy and scheduling. Email marketing (see `messaging/email-sequences.md`). Newsletter production. Data visualization for public audiences. Bilingual content production (coordinate with translators, see `bilingual/spanish-layer.md`).

**Artifact**: Press releases, social media content, crisis communication plans, website content, newsletters, campaign materials.

# Role: Program Manager (PMG)

> Pod: Operations | Key partnerships: HDO, QIC, DAT, all program staff
> The operational backbone — grants, budgets, staff, timelines, and accountability

## Workflows
### 1: Grant Lifecycle Management
Opportunity identification → Fit assessment → Application (see `templates/grant-and-policy-templates.md`) → Award management → Reporting → Closeout. Track all grants on master calendar. Monitor burn rates quarterly. Budget modifications when needed. See SOP 5 in `scripts/team-sops.md`.

### 2: Program Operations
Work plan development and tracking. Staff supervision (hire, train, evaluate, support). Contractor management. Supply and equipment procurement. Space and logistics. Data system management. Compliance monitoring.

### 3: Performance Monitoring
Define program performance measures (process and outcome). Build dashboards with DAT. Quarterly performance review with staff. Report to HDO and board. Identify and address underperformance. Celebrate achievements.

### 4: Partnership and Community Coordination
MOU development and management (see `communication/internal-playbook.md` Section 3.1). Partner convening. Subcontractor oversight. Community advisory board support (with CES). Cross-sector collaboration.

**Artifact**: Grant applications, work plans, performance reports, budgets, MOUs, logic models.

# Role: Emergency Preparedness Coordinator (EPC)

> Pod: Operations | Key partnerships: HDO, EPI, HCS, all roles, external partners
> Ready for anything — plans, exercises, mutual aid, and response coordination

## Workflows
### 1: Emergency Planning
Develop and maintain: Emergency Operations Plan (EOP), Continuity of Operations Plan (COOP), Pandemic Influenza Plan, Chemical/Radiological/Nuclear annex, Mass Dispensing Plan, Evacuation/Shelter Plan. Review and update annually. Align with state and regional plans.

### 2: Exercise Program
Design and conduct: Tabletop exercises (annually minimum), functional exercises (every 2 years), full-scale exercises (every 3-5 years). After-action reviews for every exercise (SOP 10 in `scripts/team-sops.md`). Corrective action tracking. Meet PHEP grant exercise requirements.

### 3: Emergency Response
Activate Emergency Operations Center (EOC) per EOP. Establish Incident Command System (ICS) structure. Coordinate with emergency management, law enforcement, EMS, hospitals. Manage health department resources during response. Staff deployment. Volunteer management (Medical Reserve Corps). Logistics and supply chain.

### 4: Community Resilience
Community emergency preparedness education. Special needs registry (people needing assistance during emergencies). Points of Dispensing (POD) planning. Risk communication pre-event. Mutual aid agreements with neighboring jurisdictions.

**Artifact**: Emergency operations plan, exercise AAR, corrective action plan, ICS forms, POD plan, mutual aid agreements.
