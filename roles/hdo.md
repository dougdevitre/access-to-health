# Role: Health Director (HDO)

> Pod: Policy & Leadership | Key partnerships: ALL roles, board, elected officials, partners
> The strategic leader — sets direction, holds authority, ensures accountability

---

## Role Summary

The health director leads the health department, sets strategic direction, exercises emergency public health authority, manages the budget, develops the workforce, and serves as the public face of public health. Every major decision flows through or from the HDO.

**Core competencies**: Strategic planning, public health law, emergency management, budget/finance, workforce development, community engagement, media communication, political navigation, systems thinking

**Qualifications**: Typically MPH, MD/DO, or equivalent. Many states require specific credentials for health officer authority (e.g., physician for quarantine/isolation orders).

## 5 Core Workflows

### Workflow 1: Strategic Direction and Governance

**Board of Health management**:
- Prepare monthly board packets (`communication/internal-playbook.md` Section 2.1)
- Present data-informed recommendations for board action
- Manage board relationships (individual briefings before contentious votes)
- Ensure board understands their governance role vs. operational management
- Support board in community advocacy for public health

**Strategic planning cycle** (every 3-5 years):
1. Environmental scan: Internal capacity, external trends, community needs
2. CHNA/CHIP alignment: Strategic plan built on community-identified priorities
3. Goal setting: 3-5 strategic priorities with measurable objectives
4. Resource alignment: Budget and staff allocated to strategic priorities
5. Performance monitoring: Quarterly progress review with division leads
6. Annual revision: Adjust based on progress and emerging needs

**Accreditation leadership**: Champion PHAB accreditation as organizational priority. Ensure prerequisites are current (CHNA, CHIP, strategic plan, performance management system). Allocate resources for documentation and site visit preparation. See `workflows/cross-role-workflows-expanded.md` Workflow 7.

### Workflow 2: Emergency Authority and Crisis Leadership

**Legal authority** (Missouri — verify current statutes):
- Isolation and quarantine orders (RSMo 192.020)
- Emergency health orders (restaurant closures, water advisories, evacuation)
- Disease investigation authority
- Facility inspection and enforcement
- Emergency declarations (coordinate with local government)

**Crisis leadership protocol**:

| Phase | HDO Actions |
|-------|-------------|
| **Detection** | Receive initial notification from EPI. Assess severity. Activate appropriate workflow |
| **Assessment** | Convene incident management team. Review data. Determine communication strategy |
| **Decision** | Issue orders if needed (isolation, closure, advisory). Approve all public communications |
| **Communication** | Serve as primary spokesperson or designate. Brief elected officials. Brief board |
| **Management** | Oversee response operations. Ensure resource allocation. Monitor staff well-being |
| **Recovery** | Transition from response to recovery. After-action review. Corrective actions. Public update |

**Key principle**: The HDO approves ALL external communications during a crisis. No staff member speaks to media or issues public statements without HDO authorization.

**Escalation to HDO** (from any role):
- Any death associated with a reportable condition
- ≥2 linked cases (outbreak)
- Novel or unusual pathogen
- Media inquiry during active investigation
- Legal action or threat
- Significant budget variance
- Staff safety concern
- Political/community controversy

### Workflow 3: Budget and Financial Management

**Annual budget cycle**:

| Month | Activity |
|-------|----------|
| Jan-Feb | Division leads submit budget requests aligned to strategic plan |
| Mar | HDO reviews, prioritizes, and drafts proposed budget |
| Apr | Board review and community input |
| May | Board adoption |
| Jun | Budget implementation for new fiscal year |
| Quarterly | Budget vs. actual review with division leads |
| Ongoing | Grant applications, revenue diversification, expenditure monitoring |

**Revenue management**:
- Federal grants: Track award terms, reporting deadlines, expenditure rates
- State contracts: Negotiate scope, deliverables, payment terms
- Local tax revenue: Advocate for public health investment with elected officials
- Fee revenue: Update fee schedules for cost recovery
- Foundation grants: Build relationships, pursue aligned opportunities

**Financial principles**:
- No single revenue source >30% of operating budget (diversification)
- Maintain 2-3 months operating reserve
- Indirect cost rate negotiated and applied to all eligible grants
- Community advisory board compensated for participation
- See `references/fiscal-crisis-brief.md` for sustainability framework

### Workflow 4: Workforce Development

**Workforce challenges** (ongoing):
- Recruitment: Compete with healthcare and private sector salaries
- Retention: Burnout, low pay, limited advancement opportunities
- Diversity: Staff should reflect community demographics
- Succession: Knowledge transfer before retirements, cross-training
- Pipeline: Internships, fellowships, loan repayment programs

**HDO workforce actions**:

| Action | Frequency |
|--------|-----------|
| Workforce assessment (gaps, vacancies, turnover) | Annually |
| Salary benchmarking (vs. comparable positions) | Every 2 years |
| Staff satisfaction survey | Annually |
| Professional development budget allocation | Annually |
| Succession planning (for key positions) | Ongoing |
| Intern/fellowship program management | Semester |
| Staff recognition and retention initiatives | Monthly |
| Burnout and wellness check-ins | Quarterly |

**Staff meeting leadership**: Monthly all-staff meetings per framework in `communication/internal-playbook.md` Section 1.4. Be visible, be honest, recognize contributions, share information.

### Workflow 5: External Relations and Advocacy

**Elected officials**:
- Schedule regular briefings (quarterly minimum) with key legislators and council members
- Provide data on constituent health needs
- Testify on health-related legislation
- Build relationships before you need something
- Use BLUF format for briefings (`communication/internal-playbook.md` Section 2.3)

**Partner organizations**:
- Hospital systems: CHNA/CHIP alignment, community benefit, data sharing
- Academic institutions: Research partnerships, workforce pipeline, evaluation
- Community organizations: Program delivery, community engagement, trust
- Other government agencies: Health in All Policies, cross-sector collaboration
- Business community: Workplace wellness, economic impact of health

**Media relations**:
- Serve as primary spokesperson (or designate HCS as spokesperson with HDO approval)
- Proactive media engagement: Tell your story before budget season
- Reactive media: Follow `communication/external-playbook.md` protocols
- Op-ed writing: Position health department as trusted voice
- Social media: Approve crisis-related posts, support ongoing strategy

**Advocacy** (non-partisan):
- Advocate for public health funding and infrastructure
- Support evidence-based policies (without partisan framing)
- Testify at legislative hearings
- Participate in professional associations (NACCHO, ASTHO, state affiliate)
- See `features/advocacy-toolkit.md` for templates and strategies

## Decision Framework

**When to act unilaterally** (HDO authority):
- Imminent threat to public health (emergency orders)
- Personnel decisions within authority
- Operational decisions within approved budget
- Media responses during active crisis

**When to consult the board**:
- Budget adoption or amendment
- Policy changes
- Strategic direction
- Organizational restructuring
- Controversial public positions

**When to consult legal counsel**:
- Isolation/quarantine orders (due process requirements)
- FOIA requests with sensitive content
- Litigation or potential litigation
- Contracts and MOUs
- Personnel disputes
- Regulatory enforcement actions
