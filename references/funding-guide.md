## Grant Writing Quick Reference

### Pre-Application Checklist
- [ ] NOFO/RFP reviewed in full (not just summary)
- [ ] Eligibility confirmed (organization type, geography, past funding)
- [ ] Internal approval to apply (leadership sign-off)
- [ ] Grant team assembled (lead writer, budget person, data person, program expert)
- [ ] Timeline built backwards from deadline
- [ ] SAM.gov registration current (federal grants require this)
- [ ] UEI number active
- [ ] Indirect cost rate negotiated (or de minimis 10%)
- [ ] Letters of support requested (allow 2+ weeks)
- [ ] Data compiled (needs assessment, disparity data, program results)

### Narrative Structure (Standard Federal Grant)
1. **Need statement** (20-25% of narrative)
   - Local data with disparities identified
   - Structural/systemic framing (not individual blame)
   - Target population description
   - Gap between need and current services
2. **Project design** (30-35% of narrative)
   - Goals, objectives, activities (SMART format)
   - Evidence base for approach
   - Logic model or theory of change
   - Timeline with milestones
3. **Organizational capacity** (15-20% of narrative)
   - Relevant experience
   - Staff qualifications
   - Partnerships and collaboration
   - Infrastructure (data systems, facilities)
4. **Evaluation plan** (10-15% of narrative)
   - Process measures (did we do what we said?)
   - Outcome measures (did it make a difference?)
   - Data collection methods and timeline
   - Use of evaluation findings for improvement
5. **Sustainability** (5-10% of narrative)
   - How will this continue after grant funding ends?
   - Diversified funding strategy
   - Policy or systems change that outlasts the grant
   - Community ownership

### Budget Best Practices
- Personnel costs should align with effort described in narrative
- Include indirect costs (don't leave money on the table)
- Budget justification should explain every line item
- Include community compensation for advisory boards, focus groups, etc.
- Include translation/interpretation costs for multilingual programs
- Include evaluation costs (10-15% of total is common for federal grants)
