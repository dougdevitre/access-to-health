## 1. Health Equity and Social Justice

**APHA Position**: Health equity is a core value. Health disparities are caused by structural racism, poverty, and discrimination — not individual behavior or genetics.

**Key Evidence**:
- Racism is a public health crisis (APHA declaration, 2020)
- Social determinants account for 80-90% of health outcomes
- Health equity requires addressing root causes, not just symptoms
- Intersectionality: Multiple forms of oppression compound health effects

**Relevance**: Guardrail #3 (equity lens). All data disaggregated by race, ethnicity, income, geography.

## 2. Racism and Health

**APHA Position**: Racism is a driving force of the social determinants of health. Structural racism operates through housing, education, employment, criminal justice, and healthcare systems.

**Key Evidence**:
- Weathering: Chronic stress from racism accelerates biological aging
- Implicit bias affects clinical decision-making (pain management, referrals, diagnostic assumptions)
- Residential segregation is the fundamental cause of racial health disparities
- Anti-racist policies improve population health

**Relevance**: `populations/deep-dives/black-african-american.md`, all population contexts

## 3. Maternal and Child Health

**APHA Position**: Every person deserves a safe, healthy pregnancy regardless of race, income, or geography.

**Key Evidence**:
- US maternal mortality is highest among wealthy nations
- Black women die at 3-4x the rate of white women (structural racism, not biology)
- Midwifery and doula care reduce complications and disparities
- Postpartum Medicaid extension (12 months) reduces maternal mortality
- Home visiting programs (NFP, PAT) improve outcomes

**Relevance**: `workflows/cross-role-workflows-expanded.md` Workflow 3, `messaging/campaign-builder.md` Campaign 3

## 4. Substance Use and Harm Reduction

**APHA Position**: Substance use disorders are treatable health conditions, not moral failures. Harm reduction saves lives.

**Key Evidence**:
- Naloxone reverses opioid overdose and is safe for community distribution
- Syringe service programs reduce HIV/HCV without increasing drug use
- Medications for opioid use disorder (methadone, buprenorphine, naltrexone) are first-line treatment
- Criminalization worsens health outcomes and increases racial disparities
- Fentanyl test strips are a lifesaving harm reduction tool

**Relevance**: `workflows/cross-role-workflows-expanded.md` Workflow 4, `messaging/campaign-builder.md` Campaign 2

## 5. Mental Health

**APHA Position**: Mental health is inseparable from physical health. Parity in coverage and access is a right.

**Key Evidence**:
- 1 in 5 adults experiences mental illness annually
- Only 46% of adults with mental illness receive treatment
- Disparities in access: Black and Latino populations less likely to receive treatment
- 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline provides 24/7 free support
- School-based mental health services improve access for youth

**Relevance**: `messaging/campaign-builder.md` Campaign 4, `roles/remaining-roles.md` BHC role

## 6. Gun Violence Prevention

**APHA Position**: Gun violence is a public health emergency. Evidence-based prevention works.

**Key Evidence**:
- Firearms are the leading cause of death for children and adolescents
- Gun violence disproportionately affects Black communities
- Extreme risk protection orders (red flag laws) reduce suicide
- Community violence intervention programs reduce shootings
- Public health approach: surveillance, research, prevention, policy

## 7. Climate Change and Health

**APHA Position**: Climate change is the greatest public health threat of the 21st century. Health departments must prepare.

**Key Evidence**:
- Heat-related illness and death increasing (disproportionate impact on elderly, outdoor workers, low-income)
- Air quality worsening (asthma, COPD, cardiovascular effects)
- Vector-borne disease range expanding (Lyme, West Nile, Zika)
- Mental health impacts (eco-anxiety, climate grief, disaster trauma)
- Food and water security threatened

**Relevance**: Environmental health workflows, emergency preparedness

## 8. Tobacco and E-Cigarettes

**APHA Position**: Tobacco is the leading preventable cause of death. E-cigarettes are not safe, especially for youth.

**Key Evidence**:
- Tobacco kills 480,000 Americans annually
- Secondhand smoke causes disease in nonsmokers
- E-cigarette use among youth is an epidemic
- Comprehensive tobacco control works: taxes, smoke-free laws, cessation services, counter-marketing
- Quitlines are effective and free

## 9. Immunization

**APHA Position**: Vaccines are safe, effective, and essential. Vaccine hesitancy is a public health threat.

**Key Evidence**:
- Vaccines prevent 2-3 million deaths globally per year
- Community immunity requires high vaccination rates (>90% for most diseases)
- Vaccine hesitancy is driven by misinformation, distrust, and access barriers — not primarily by ideology
- Trust-based approaches (HEAR framework) are more effective than mandates alone

**Relevance**: `messaging/campaign-builder.md` Campaign 1, `messaging/trust-rebuilding-playbook.md`

## 10. Food Systems and Nutrition

**APHA Position**: Access to affordable, nutritious food is a human right. Food insecurity is a health issue.

**Key Evidence**:
- 44 million Americans experience food insecurity
- Food deserts are concentrated in low-income and minority neighborhoods
- SNAP participation reduces food insecurity by 30%
- WIC improves birth outcomes and child nutrition
- Double Up Food Bucks increases fruit/vegetable consumption

**Relevance**: `features/resource-navigator.md` Domain 2, `messaging/campaign-builder.md` Campaign 5

## 11. Housing and Health

**APHA Position**: Safe, stable, affordable housing is a social determinant of health. Homelessness is a public health crisis.

**Key Evidence**:
- Housing instability increases ED visits, hospitalizations, and mortality
- Lead paint in pre-1978 housing causes irreversible brain damage in children
- Housing First reduces homelessness and improves health outcomes
- Eviction is associated with depression, suicide, substance use, and poor birth outcomes

**Relevance**: `features/resource-navigator.md` Domain 1, `messaging/campaign-builder.md` Campaign 6

## 12. Environmental Health and Justice

**APHA Position**: Environmental hazards are unequally distributed. Environmental justice is health justice.

**Key Evidence**:
- Communities of color breathe 56% more pollution than they produce
- Lead exposure affects 500,000+ children annually in the US
- Proximity to industrial facilities correlates with race more than income
- Environmental regulations save lives and reduce healthcare costs

**Relevance**: `workflows/cross-role-workflows-expanded.md` Workflow 5

## 13. Health Workforce

**APHA Position**: The public health workforce is critically underfunded and understaffed.

**Key Evidence**:
- Public health workforce has declined 20% since 2008
- Estimated shortage of 80,000 public health workers
- Burnout rates increased significantly post-COVID
- Workforce does not reflect community demographics
- CHW integration improves outcomes and reduces costs

**Relevance**: `references/fiscal-crisis-brief.md` Pillar 3
