# Fiscal Crisis Brief — 5-Pillar Unified Health Blueprint

> A framework for sustaining public health in an era of budget cuts
> For health directors, board members, elected officials, and advocates

---

## The Crisis

Public health infrastructure is chronically underfunded. Local health departments face:
- **Federal uncertainty**: Grant cycles, continuing resolutions, and programmatic cuts
- **State budget pressure**: Competing priorities, tax limitations, unfunded mandates
- **Local revenue limits**: Property tax caps, declining tax bases in rural areas, political resistance to health levies
- **Workforce exodus**: Burnout, low pay, retirement wave, competition with private sector
- **Rising demand**: Pandemic recovery, behavioral health crisis, opioid epidemic, chronic disease burden

**Result**: Departments are asked to do more with less, then blamed when communities are unhealthy.

---

## The 5-Pillar Blueprint

### Pillar 1: Diversify Revenue

**Strategy**: No single funding source should represent more than 30% of operating budget.

| Revenue Source | Strategy | Timeline |
|---------------|----------|----------|
| Federal grants | Maintain CDC, HRSA, SAMHSA cooperative agreements. Pursue new NOFOs aligned with capacity | Ongoing |
| State contracts | Advocate for increased state investment in local public health | Legislative session |
| Local tax levy | Pursue dedicated health levy (property tax, sales tax) with community support | 12-18 months |
| Fee revenue | Update fee schedules for permits, licenses, inspections (cost recovery, not profit) | 6 months |
| Foundation grants | Build relationships with health-focused foundations (see `references/funding-guide.md`) | Ongoing |
| Medicaid billing | Bill for eligible clinical and care coordination services (CHW services now billable in many states) | 6-12 months |
| Partnerships | Shared services agreements with hospital systems, academic institutions, neighboring jurisdictions | 12 months |

### Pillar 2: Demonstrate Value

**Strategy**: If you can't show what you do, you can't defend your budget.

**Actions**:
1. **Return on Investment (ROI) calculator**: For every $1 invested in [program], the community saves $[X] in [healthcare costs, productivity, etc.]
2. **Public dashboard**: Visible, updated, plain-language data on what the health department does and what it costs
3. **Annual report**: Professional, public-facing, infographic style (see `artifacts/reporting-templates.md`)
4. **Media engagement**: Proactive — tell your story before budget season, not after cuts are proposed
5. **Community voice**: Let community members and partners testify about the value of health department services

**Key data points to maintain**:
- People served per year (by program)
- Diseases prevented (immunizations, investigations)
- Dollars saved (ROI calculations)
- Access created (screenings, referrals, enrollments)
- Disparities addressed (equity metrics)

### Pillar 3: Build Workforce Resilience

**Strategy**: Protect and develop the workforce — they ARE the infrastructure.

| Challenge | Strategy |
|-----------|----------|
| Low pay | Advocate for competitive salaries (benchmark against hospital and state comparables) |
| Burnout | Workload management, mental health support (EAP), flexible schedules |
| Recruitment | Pipeline programs (internships, fellowships, PHAP, loan repayment — NHSC) |
| Retention | Professional development, career ladders, recognition, meaningful work |
| Succession | Cross-training, knowledge transfer, leadership development for mid-career staff |
| Diversity | Recruit staff that reflect the community. Community health workers from the community |

### Pillar 4: Forge Strategic Partnerships

**Strategy**: Public health cannot work alone. Build formal partnerships that share resources, data, and accountability.

**Priority Partners**:
- **Hospital systems**: CHNA/CHIP alignment, community benefit spending, data sharing, co-funding
- **Medicaid managed care**: Population health management, care coordination, shared savings
- **Academic institutions**: Research, evaluation, workforce pipeline, grant partnerships
- **Community organizations**: Program delivery, community engagement, trust, cultural competency
- **Other government**: Housing, education, transportation, justice, economic development (Health in All Policies)
- **Business community**: Workplace wellness, economic impact of health, corporate partnerships

**Formalize everything**: MOUs, data sharing agreements, joint strategic plans (see `communication/internal-playbook.md` Section 3)

### Pillar 5: Advocate for Systemic Change

**Strategy**: The current funding model is broken. Advocate for structural reform.

**Policy Priorities**:
1. **Dedicated public health funding**: Statutory minimum investment in public health infrastructure (not dependent on annual appropriations)
2. **Modernized funding formulas**: Population-based, equity-weighted, adjusted for health burden (not just population count)
3. **Cross-sector investment**: Health-in-all-policies approach that directs non-health dollars toward health outcomes
4. **Medicaid parity**: Ensure community-based prevention and CHW services are billable and reimbursed at sustainable rates
5. **Workforce investment**: Loan repayment, competitive pay mandates, pipeline programs

**Advocacy tactics**: See `features/advocacy-toolkit.md`

## Making the Case: Talking Points

### For Elected Officials
- "Public health is the most cost-effective investment in community health. For every $1 spent on childhood immunization, society saves $10.90."
- "Your constituents use our services: [#] immunizations, [#] screenings, [#] inspections last year."
- "When you cut public health, the costs don't disappear — they shift to emergency departments, lost productivity, and preventable death."

### For Board Members
- "Our budget has [increased/decreased/remained flat] by [%] over [X] years, while demand has increased by [%]."
- "We are [X]% dependent on [funding source]. If that source decreases, we lose [specific programs]."
- "Our staff vacancy rate is [%]. Every unfilled position represents services not delivered."

### For Community Members
- "The health department prevents disease before you get sick, protects your food and water, and connects families to resources."
- "We served [#] people last year with a budget of $[X] — that's $[Y] per person served."
- "When public health works, you don't see it. When it fails, everyone pays."
