# Missouri Public Health — Infrastructure, Data, and Programs

> State-specific reference for Missouri public health professionals
> Updated regularly — verify current data before citing

## Missouri Public Health Infrastructure

### State Agency
- **Missouri Department of Health and Senior Services (DHSS)**
  - Division of Community and Public Health
  - Division of Senior and Disability Services
  - Division of Regulation and Licensure
  - State Public Health Laboratory
  - Office of Epidemiology

### Local Health Department Structure
- Missouri has **114 local public health agencies** (LPHAs)
  - City health departments, county health departments, and combined city-county agencies
  - Home rule charter cities may operate independent health departments
  - Some counties share services through multi-county health departments
- Funding: Mix of local tax revenue, state general revenue, federal grants, and fees
- Governance: Board of Health (appointed) or county commission oversight

### Key Missouri Health Indicators

**Life Expectancy** (approximate, verify current year)
- State average: ~77 years
- Significant variation by county (70-82 years) and census tract (up to 20-year gaps within metro areas)

**Leading Causes of Death**
1. Heart disease
2. Cancer
3. Accidents (unintentional injuries)
4. Chronic lower respiratory disease
5. Stroke
6. Alzheimer's disease
7. Diabetes
8. Drug overdose (within accidents — separately tracked)

**Maternal and Child Health**
- Infant mortality rate: ~6-7 per 1,000 live births (verify current)
- Black infant mortality: ~2.5x white infant mortality
- Preterm birth rate: ~10-11%
- Low birth weight: ~8-9%
- Teen birth rate: Declining but above national average

**Substance Use**
- Opioid overdose deaths: Significant increase since 2015; fentanyl dominant
- Methamphetamine: Major issue in rural Missouri
- Alcohol: Above national average for binge drinking in some regions
- Naloxone standing order: Available statewide

**Communicable Disease**
- STI rates: Chlamydia and gonorrhea above national average in urban areas
- HIV: Concentrated in St. Louis and Kansas City metro areas
- TB: Low incidence but ongoing surveillance required
- Hepatitis C: Elevated, particularly in PWID populations

## St. Louis Metropolitan Area

### The Delmar Divide
- Life expectancy differs by up to **18 years** across a single street (Delmar Blvd)
- North of Delmar: Predominantly Black, higher poverty, lower life expectancy
- South of Delmar: Predominantly white, higher income, higher life expectancy
- This is not coincidence — it is the legacy of residential segregation, redlining, disinvestment, and structural racism

### St. Louis City Health Data
- Population: ~290,000 (city) / ~2.8 million (metro)
- City-county separation (since 1876) creates jurisdictional complexity
- Among the highest rates of: violent crime, infant mortality, STIs, lead poisoning in the nation
- Significant concentration of poverty in north city/north county

## Rural Missouri

### Challenges
- **Provider shortages**: Many rural counties are Health Professional Shortage Areas (HPSAs) and Medically Underserved Areas (MUAs)
- **Hospital closures**: Multiple rural hospital closures in recent years
- **Transportation**: Distances to specialty care can be 60+ miles
- **Broadband**: Limited internet access impacts telehealth, health information, and economic opportunity
- **Methamphetamine**: Disproportionate impact in rural communities
- **Agricultural health**: Pesticide exposure, occupational injuries, stress

### Rural Resources
- **OATS Transit**: Rural transportation network
- **Missouri Telehealth Network**: University of Missouri system
- **Area Health Education Centers (AHEC)**: Clinical training in underserved areas
- **Missouri Primary Care Association**: FQHC support
- **Extension Service**: University of Missouri Extension — community health education

### Emergency Powers
- **Health Director**: Authority to issue isolation and quarantine orders (RSMo 192.020)
- **Governor**: State of emergency declaration
- **Local orders**: Board of health may adopt rules and regulations with force of law
- Due process requirements: Right to hearing, legal representation, least restrictive means
