## Workflow 1: Disease Outbreak Response (Expanded)

### Decision Tree
```
Report received (lab, provider, school, community)
  │
  ├─ Single case of highly infectious disease (measles, meningitis, Ebola)?
  │   └─ YES → Immediate activation. Skip to Control Measures. Notify state EPI within 1 hour.
  │
  ├─ ≥2 cases with epidemiologic link?
  │   └─ YES → Outbreak investigation. Proceed to Step 1.
  │
  └─ Single case, routine reportable?
      └─ Standard case investigation (EPI Workflow 2). Monitor for additional cases.
```

### Timeline
| Hour | Action | Responsible | Communication |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Report received, initial assessment | EPI | Notify EPI supervisor |
| 0-2 | Verify cases, establish preliminary case definition | EPI | Notify HDO |
| 2-4 | Assemble investigation team | EPI | Email/call to EHS, PHN, DIS, lab |
| 4-8 | Begin case interviews, launch line list, collect specimens | EPI + team | Nothing public yet |
| 8-12 | Preliminary analysis (epi curve, attack rate, exposure assessment) | EPI + DAT | Internal SitRep #1 to HDO |
| 12-24 | Implement control measures | EHS + PHN | Provider notification (HCS drafts, HDO approves) |
| 24-48 | Environmental assessment if applicable | EHS | Inspection report |
| 24-48 | Public communication if warranted | HCS | Media statement (HDO approval required) |
| 48-72 | Expanded investigation (case-control study if needed) | EPI | SitRep #2 |
| 72+ | Monitor for new cases, evaluate control measures | EPI | Daily or weekly SitReps until resolved |
| Resolution | Final investigation report, AAR | EPI + EPC | Distribute to partners, file |

### Communication Templates

**Internal SitRep (for HDO)**:
Subject: Outbreak SitRep #[#] — [Condition] at [Location]
Cases: [#] confirmed, [#] probable, [#] suspect
Epi summary: [1-2 sentences]
Control measures: [Status]
Next steps: [Actions planned]
Decision needed: [If applicable]

**Provider Notification**:
Subject: Clinical Advisory: [Condition] Cluster in [Area]
[1 paragraph: What is happening]
[1 paragraph: Clinical guidance — testing, treatment, reporting]
[Reporting requirements and contact]

**Parent/Community Notification**:
Subject: Health Advisory: [Condition] at [School/Location]
[What happened — factual, brief]
[What we're doing about it]
[What you should do / watch for]
[When we'll update you next]
[Contact for questions]

**Media Holding Statement**:
"[Health Department] is investigating reports of [condition] at [location]. We are working with [partners] to identify the source and implement control measures. The health and safety of our community is our top priority. We will provide an update by [time]. For the latest information: [website] [phone]."

### Documentation Checklist
□ Line list (updated continuously)
□ Epi curve (updated daily during active investigation)
□ Investigation timeline
□ Interview questionnaires (blank + completed)
□ Lab results
□ Environmental assessment report (if applicable)
□ SitReps (numbered, dated)
□ All communications sent (provider, public, media)
□ Control measures implemented (with dates)
□ Final investigation report
□ After-action review

### Escalation Criteria
- ≥1 death → Notify state EPI and HDO immediately. Media plan activated
- Novel pathogen or unusual presentation → CDC Emergency Operations Center (770-488-7100)
- Multi-jurisdictional → State coordination required
- School/childcare → State education department notification
- Food establishment → EHS closure authority. FDA if interstate product
- Healthcare facility → CMS/state licensure notification

## Workflow 2: Community Health Needs Assessment (Expanded)

### Decision Tree
```
CHNA trigger
  │
  ├─ 3-year hospital CHNA cycle (IRS requirement)?
  │   └─ Partner with hospital system(s). Share data, costs, community engagement.
  │
  ├─ Health department strategic planning cycle?
  │   └─ Internal lead. May partner with hospitals and/or academic institutions.
  │
  └─ Grant requirement or special assessment?
      └─ Scope to funder requirements. May be topic-specific (e.g., opioid, maternal health).
```

### Timeline (12-month process)
| Month | Phase | Lead | Activities |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1-2 | Planning | PMG | Define scope, budget, partnership MOUs, advisory committee, community engagement plan |
| 2-4 | Quantitative data | EPI + DAT | Compile health status data from MICA, PLACES, BRFSS, ACS, vital statistics, program data. Analyze by race, ethnicity, income, geography |
| 3-6 | Community engagement | CES + CHW | 8-12 focus groups, 500+ surveys (multilingual), 15-20 key informant interviews, 2-3 community forums. Ensure representation of underserved populations |
| 6-8 | Analysis | EPI + CES | Integrate quantitative and qualitative. Identify themes, disparities, assets. Draft findings |
| 8-9 | Priority setting | CES + advisory board | Community-driven prioritization. Present findings to 3+ community groups. Voting/ranking exercise |
| 9-10 | CHIP development | PMG + all leads | Develop Community Health Improvement Plan: 3-5 priorities, measurable objectives, strategies, responsible leads, timelines |
| 10-11 | Report | DAT + HCS | Write CHNA report (plain language version + full technical version). Design community-facing summary |
| 11-12 | Release and implementation | HDO + PMG | Public release. Community presentation. Begin CHIP implementation. Post on website |

### Community Engagement Minimum Standards
- ≥8 focus groups across diverse populations (race, geography, age, language)
- ≥500 survey responses with demographic representation matching community
- ≥15 key informant interviews (community leaders, providers, residents, people with lived experience)
- ≥2 open community forums in accessible locations with interpretation and childcare
- Advisory committee meets ≥6 times during process
- Report back to community within 30 days of completion
- Community members compensated for participation ($25-50/focus group, $10/survey)

## Workflow 3: Maternal Mortality Case Review (Expanded)

### Decision Tree
```
Maternal death reported (within 1 year of pregnancy)
  │
  ├─ Within 42 days of end of pregnancy?
  │   └─ Direct obstetric death. ICD-10 classification. Priority for MMRC review.
  │
  ├─ 43 days to 1 year post-pregnancy?
  │   └─ Late maternal death. Include in review (especially mental health/substance use deaths).
  │
  └─ Pregnancy-associated but not pregnancy-related (e.g., car accident)?
      └─ Review for preventability. May still have pregnancy-contributing factors.
```

### Timeline
| Timeframe | Action | Responsible |
|---|---|---|
| 0-48 hours | Death notification received. Notify MMRC chair | MCH |
| 1-4 weeks | Medical record request from all providers (hospital, OB, PCP, ED, EMS, coroner) | EPI |
| 4-8 weeks | Case abstraction. De-identify. Prepare summary | MCH + EPI |
| 8-12 weeks | MMRC meeting. Review case. Determine: Was it pregnancy-related? Was it preventable? What were contributing factors? | Full committee |
| Post-review | Document recommendations. Assign action items. Track implementation | MCH + HDO |
| Annually | Aggregate report (de-identified). Community presentation | MCH + CES |

### MMRC Composition
Obstetrics, maternal-fetal medicine, midwifery, anesthesiology, nursing, social work, behavioral health, community doula, community member with lived experience, EPI, pathology, EMS. Minimum 10 members. Community representation mandatory.

## Workflow 4: Substance Use Crisis Response (Expanded)

### Decision Tree
```
Overdose spike detected
  │
  ├─ ≥3 overdoses in 24 hours in same geographic area?
  │   └─ ACTIVATE crisis response. Deploy naloxone saturation.
  │
  ├─ Unusual substance identified (novel fentanyl analog, xylazine, nitazene)?
  │   └─ ACTIVATE + drug alert. Notify DEA, state lab, harm reduction networks.
  │
  └─ Gradual increase over weeks?
      └─ Enhanced monitoring. Prevention intensification. Treatment access expansion.
```

### Timeline (Acute Spike)
| Hour | Action | Responsible |
|---|---|---|
| 0-4 | Verify spike (EMS data, coroner, ED, syndromic surveillance) | EPI + DAT |
| 0-4 | Deploy street outreach with naloxone, fentanyl test strips | SUP + CHW |
| 2-6 | Issue public health advisory (spike alert) | HCS (HDO approval) |
| 2-8 | Expand low-barrier MAT access (walk-in, same-day start) | BHC |
| 4-12 | Provider notification (clinical advisory to EDs, PCPs) | HCS |
| 12-24 | Community notification (social media, partner agencies) | HCS + CES |
| 24-48 | Community forum if multiple deaths | CES + BHC |
| 48-72 | Grief support resources deployed | BHC + faith partners |
| 1-2 weeks | Analysis: Source identification, demographic patterns, geographic clusters | EPI |
| Ongoing | Policy advocacy (harm reduction funding, opioid settlement allocation) | POL + HDO |

## Workflow 5: Environmental Justice Investigation (Expanded)

### Timeline
| Phase | Duration | Lead | Actions |
|---|---|---|---|
| Community report | Week 1 | CES | Receive concern, validate through listening session, document |
| Data assessment | Weeks 2-6 | EPI + DAT | Cancer registry analysis, exposure assessment, spatial analysis, EJScreen review |
| Environmental sampling | Weeks 4-10 | EHS | Air, water, soil sampling. Facility permit review. Historical contamination research |
| Health study design | Weeks 6-12 | EPI + academic partner | Exposure assessment, health survey, biomonitoring if warranted |
| Community meeting | Week 12 | CES + HCS + EPI | Present preliminary findings (plain language, bilingual). Answer questions. Listen |
| Full investigation | Months 4-12 | EPI + EHS | Complete health study. Environmental characterization |
| Final report | Month 12-14 | EPI | Technical report + community summary (plain language) |
| Advocacy | Ongoing | POL + CES | Remediation demands, regulatory enforcement, policy change, health monitoring |

## Workflow 6: Immigrant/Refugee Health Integration (Expanded)

### Timeline
| Phase | Action | Responsible |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-arrival (2-4 weeks) | Cultural competency training for staff. Interpreter arrangements. Clinical protocol review. Supply procurement | PMG + PHN |
| Arrival (Week 1) | Domestic health screening: TB (IGRA), hepatitis B, parasites, lead (children), immunization review, mental health screening (RHS-15), dental, vision | PHN + NUT + BHC |
| Week 1-2 | Assign culturally matched CHW. Orient to health system, pharmacy, emergency procedures. Insurance enrollment (Refugee Medical Assistance first 8 months → Medicaid) | CHW |
| Month 1-3 | Primary care linkage. WIC enrollment. Immunization catch-up. Dental referral. Behavioral health assessment | PHN + NUT + OHC + BHC |
| Month 3-6 | Chronic disease screening and management. Follow-up on screening findings. Ongoing navigation | PHN + CHW |
| Month 6-12 | Ongoing care coordination. Community integration support. Employment health screening | CHW + CES |
| Year 1+ | Transition to routine care. Monitor for acculturation stress impacts. Maintain cultural broker connection | All |

## Workflow 7: PHAB Accreditation (Expanded)

### Timeline (Initial Accreditation — 18-24 month process)
| Month | Phase | Lead | Key Activities |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1-3 | Readiness assessment | QIC | Self-assessment against 12 domains. Gap analysis. Action plan |
| 3-6 | Prerequisites | HDO + PMG | Current CHNA, CHIP, strategic plan, health department performance management system, workforce development plan. All must be <5 years old |
| 6-12 | Documentation collection | QIC + all division leads | Gather conformity documentation for each standard. 2 examples per measure minimum |
| 12-14 | Internal review | QIC | Mock site visit. Peer review of documentation. Fill gaps |
| 14-16 | Application submission | QIC | Upload to e-PHAB system. Pay application fee |
| 16-20 | PHAB review | N/A (PHAB) | PHAB reviews documentation. May request additional examples |
| 20-22 | Site visit (3 days) | QIC + all staff | PHAB team interviews staff at all levels. Verifies documentation. Tours facilities |
| 22-24 | Decision | N/A (PHAB) | Accredited / Conditional / Not accredited |
| Ongoing | Maintenance | QIC | Annual report. Reaccreditation every 5 years |

### PHAB 12 Domains Quick Reference
1. Assess/monitor population health status
2. Investigate health problems/hazards
3. Inform/educate about health issues
4. Community engagement in health issues
5. Develop public health policies/plans
6. Enforce public health laws
7. Promote access to health care
8. Maintain competent public/personal health workforce
9. Evaluate public health services
10. Contribute to/use evidence base
11. Maintain administrative/management capacity
12. Maintain governance capacity

## Workflow 8: School Suicide Cluster (Expanded)

### Decision Tree
```
Student death by suicide reported
  │
  ├─ First death in this school in 12 months?
  │   └─ Activate postvention protocol. Deploy crisis team. Monitor for contagion.
  │
  ├─ Second death in same school within 6 months?
  │   └─ CLUSTER ALERT. Activate enhanced protocol. Consult CDC suicide cluster guidelines.
  │       Contact state suicide prevention coordinator. Extended monitoring.
  │
  └─ Third+ death?
      └─ FULL CLUSTER RESPONSE. Media containment critical. Extended counseling.
          CDC technical assistance. Community-wide response.
```

### Timeline
| Hour/Day | Action | Responsible |
|---|---|---|
| 0-2 hours | Verify information. Activate postvention team | BHC + SHC |
| 0-4 hours | Deploy crisis counselors to school | BHC |
| 4-8 hours | Staff briefing: Talking points, warning signs, referral process | SHC + BHC |
| Day 1 | Student assemblies or classroom presentations (age-appropriate) | BHC + school counselors |
| Day 1 | Parent communication (letter home) — safe messaging, warning signs, resources | HCS + SHC |
| Day 1-3 | Media management: Safe reporting guidelines, no details about method | HCS + HDO |
| Day 1-7 | Individual screening of at-risk students (close friends, similar stressors) | BHC |
| Week 1-4 | Extended counseling availability. Support groups | BHC |
| Week 2-4 | Community forum (parent information night) | CES + BHC |
| Month 1-6 | Ongoing monitoring. Memorial guidance (avoid glorification). Gatekeeper training rollout | BHC + SHC + HED |

### CRITICAL SAFETY RULES
- NEVER name the method. NEVER share details of the death location or circumstances
- NEVER create a permanent memorial at the school (drives contagion). Temporary tributes only, then redirect to positive action
- Follow safe messaging guidelines: reportingonsuicide.org
- Monitor social media for contagion (RIP pages, "I want to follow" posts)
- Do NOT hold large school assemblies about the death — use small group settings
- ALL communication reviewed by someone trained in suicide postvention before distribution
