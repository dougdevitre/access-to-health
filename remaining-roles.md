# Role: Disease Intervention Specialist (DIS)

> Pod: Surveillance | Key partnerships: EPI, PHN, BHC, CHW
> Daily focus: Partner notification (STI/HIV), contact tracing (TB, outbreaks), linkage to care, field investigation

## Workflows
### 1: Partner Notification (STI/HIV)
Receive case from surveillance → Interview index case (sensitive, confidential, non-judgmental) → Elicit sexual and needle-sharing partners → Locate partners (field work, phone, social media — never reveal source) → Notify of exposure → Offer testing and treatment → Document per CDC DIS standards → Track outcomes (tested, treated, referred). **Critical skills**: Motivational interviewing, field safety, locating techniques, cultural competency, trauma-informed communication.

### 2: Contact Tracing (TB/Outbreak)
Receive case assignment from EPI → Interview case to identify close contacts → Define exposure criteria (TB: ≥8 hours cumulative airspace sharing) → Locate and evaluate contacts → Arrange testing → Monitor through window period → Provide prophylaxis when indicated → Report outcomes.

### 3: Linkage to Care (HIV)
Goal: Newly diagnosed HIV+ individuals connected to medical care within 30 days. Navigate insurance (Medicaid, Ryan White, ADAP) → Schedule first appointment → Accompany to first visit if needed → Track viral suppression → Re-engage lost-to-care clients. Measure: Time from diagnosis to first visit, viral suppression at 6 months.

### 4: Field Investigation
Locate hard-to-reach individuals in community settings. Build rapport in sensitive conversations. Maintain strict confidentiality (42 CFR Part 2 for SUD, state STI confidentiality laws). Navigate safety — buddy system, check-in protocols, situational awareness. Document all field contacts.

**Artifact**: Partner notification record, contact investigation worksheet, linkage-to-care tracker, field contact log.

# Role: WIC Nutritionist / Dietitian (NUT)

> Pod: Nutrition | Key partnerships: PHN, MCH, CHW, HED
> Daily focus: WIC certification, nutrition counseling, breastfeeding support, food prescriptions, community nutrition

## Workflows
### 1: WIC Certification and Counseling
Determine nutritional risk and eligibility → Anthropometric assessment (height, weight, hemoglobin) → Person-centered nutrition counseling → Prescribe food package tailored to participant → Issue EBT benefits → Schedule follow-up. **Key**: Respect cultural food traditions. Provide guidance that works within the family's actual food environment, not an idealized one.

### 2: Breastfeeding Support
Lactation counseling (CLC or IBCLC) → Assess latch, positioning, milk transfer → Loan breast pumps → Address barriers (return to work, cultural beliefs, family pressure, pain) → Refer to peer counselors → Track exclusive breastfeeding rates. **Goal**: Support the mother's choice. Breast is evidence-based best, but never shame formula use.

### 3: Medical Nutrition Therapy
MNT for chronic disease: diabetes (carb counting, plate method), hypertension (DASH diet), renal disease, obesity, disordered eating. Comprehensive nutrition assessment → Individualized nutrition care plan → Monitor and adjust → Coordinate with medical providers.

### 4: Community Nutrition Programs
Cooking demonstrations, farmers market SNAP/Double Up coordination, produce prescription programs (FVRx), community garden support, school nutrition advocacy, food policy council participation.

**Artifact**: WIC certification record, nutrition care plan, breastfeeding assessment, food package prescription, group education attendance.

# Role: Substance Use Prevention Specialist (SUP)

> Pod: Behavioral | Key partnerships: BHC, CHW, SHC, HED, POL
> Daily focus: Community coalitions, youth prevention, SBIRT, harm reduction, environmental strategies

## Workflows
### 1: Community Prevention Coalitions
Staff Drug Free Communities or CADCA-model coalitions → Strategic Prevention Framework (SPF): Assessment → Capacity → Planning → Implementation → Evaluation → Cultural competence and sustainability throughout. Environmental strategies: Alcohol outlet density, social host ordinances, prescription drug monitoring, retail compliance checks.

### 2: Youth Prevention Programs
Implement evidence-based curricula: LifeSkills Training, Too Good for Drugs, PROSPER, Strengthening Families. Coordinate with schools, youth organizations, juvenile justice. Train facilitators. Monitor fidelity and outcomes. **Key**: Prevention messaging must be developmentally appropriate and avoid scare tactics (they don't work).

### 3: SBIRT Implementation
Train primary care and ED staff on Screening, Brief Intervention, Referral to Treatment. Tools: AUDIT-C (alcohol), single-question screen (drugs), NIDA Quick Screen. Monitor screening rates. Coordinate referral pathways. Track treatment initiation.

### 4: Harm Reduction Operations
Naloxone distribution and training → Syringe service programs → Fentanyl test strips → Wound care → Overdose response training in community → Stigma reduction campaigns → MAT access facilitation.

**Artifact**: Coalition strategic plan, youth program fidelity report, SBIRT screening rates, naloxone distribution log, overdose reversal report.

# Role: School Health Coordinator (SHC)

> Pod: School | Key partnerships: HED, PHN, BHC, SUP, NUT
> Daily focus: Wellness policy, school-based health services, mental health, health education, chronic disease management

## Workflows
### 1: Local Wellness Policy
USDA-required for districts in federal meal programs. Address: Nutrition education, school meal standards, physical activity, marketing restrictions, health education standards. Coordinate wellness committee (parents, students, food service, nurses, admin, community). Annual assessment. Triennial USDA review.

### 2: Mandated Screenings
Vision (K,1,3,5,7), hearing (K,1,3,5,7), scoliosis (girls 5-6, boys 7-8), BMI (per district), dental (varies). Send results and referral letters to parents. Track follow-up completion. Address barriers (cost, transportation, insurance).

### 3: School Mental Health
Multi-Tiered Systems of Support (MTSS): Tier 1 (universal SEL, classroom strategies), Tier 2 (targeted groups — anxiety, grief, anger), Tier 3 (individual intervention, wraparound). Coordinate with school counselors, psychologists, social workers. Mental Health First Aid for Youth training for all staff. Crisis response protocol.

### 4: Chronic Disease Management
Individualized Health Plans (IHP) for asthma, diabetes, seizures, severe allergies, sickle cell. Train staff on emergency medications (epinephrine auto-injector, glucagon, Diastat). 504 plan coordination. Emergency care plans accessible to all staff who interact with the student.

**Artifact**: Wellness policy assessment, screening summary report, IHP, crisis response plan, immunization compliance report.

# Role: Oral Health Program Coordinator (OHC)

> Pod: Clinical | Key partnerships: PHN, SHC, CHW, POL
> Daily focus: Dental access, school sealant programs, fluoridation advocacy, oral health education

## Workflows
### 1: Dental Access Programs
Coordinate dental clinic access for uninsured/underinsured. Manage mobile dental unit. Volunteer dental events (Give Kids a Smile, Remote Area Medical). Track utilization and outcomes by demographics. Identify dental deserts. Advocate for expanded scope of practice for dental hygienists.

### 2: School Sealant Programs
Target high-risk schools (≥50% free/reduced lunch). Coordinate parental consent. Clinical operations: Dental sealant application by hygienist or EFDA. Track placement rates, retention rates, and referral for untreated decay. Report to CDC/state dental program. **Evidence**: Sealants prevent 80% of cavities in back teeth for 2 years.

### 3: Community Water Fluoridation
Support optimal fluoridation (0.7 ppm per USPHS recommendation). Provide evidence-based information to water boards. Monitor fluoridation status across jurisdiction. Counter anti-fluoridation misinformation with empathetic, science-based messaging (see trust-rebuilding-playbook.md).

### 4: Oral-Systemic Health Education
Train CHWs and health educators on the oral-systemic connection: Periodontal disease linked to cardiovascular disease, diabetes, adverse birth outcomes. Integrate oral health screening into prenatal and chronic disease programs. Advocate for dental benefits in Medicaid expansion.

**Artifact**: School sealant program report, mobile dental unit utilization report, fluoridation status map, oral health education materials.

# Role: Community Engagement Specialist (CES)

> Pod: Community | Key partnerships: CHW, HDO, POL, HCS
> Daily focus: Advisory boards, listening sessions, community assessment, participatory processes

## Workflows
### 1: Community Advisory Board Management
Recruit → Onboard → Facilitate → Compensate → Report back. Board reflects community demographics (majority community residents, not just professionals). Compensation ($25-75/meeting minimum). Real authority (decision-input, not rubber stamp). Standing agenda: Community update, program data, decision items, action items. See SOP 7 in scripts/team-sops.md.

### 2: Listening Sessions
Design → Recruit → Facilitate → Synthesize → Report back. Methods: World Café, fishbowl, storytelling circles, photovoice, community mapping. Accessibility: Interpretation, childcare, transportation, food, accessible venue, virtual option. Ensure marginalized voices centered. Report back to community within 30 days on how input was used.

### 3: Community Health Assessment (Community Side)
Lead community engagement component of CHNA: Focus groups, surveys (multilingual), key informant interviews, community forums. Ensure representation of underserved populations. Co-interpret findings with community. Community-driven priority-setting (not health department-driven). Avoid extractive data practices.

### 4: Participatory Budgeting
Implement participatory processes for resource allocation. Train community members on budget literacy and public health priorities. Facilitate deliberation. Transparent reporting on outcomes.

**Artifact**: Advisory board charter, meeting minutes, listening session report, community forum summary, CHNA engagement report, participatory budgeting results.

# Role: Quality Improvement Coordinator (QIC)

> Pod: Operations | Key partnerships: HDO, PMG, DAT, all staff
> Daily focus: Performance management, accreditation, CQI, customer satisfaction

## Workflows
### 1: Performance Management System
Maintain system aligned with PHAB Domain 9. Measures linked to strategic plan objectives. Departmental and program-level dashboards. Quarterly performance review meetings. Annual revision of measures.

### 2: PHAB Accreditation
Lead accreditation (10 domains, 30+ standards, 100+ measures). Manage documentation. Coordinate site visit. Maintain status through annual reports and 5-year reaccreditation. If not yet accredited: Gap analysis → Documentation collection → Pre-site visit mock review → Application → Site visit.

### 3: Continuous Quality Improvement (CQI)
PDSA (Plan-Do-Study-Act) methodology. Tools: Fishbone (Ishikawa) diagrams, process mapping, run charts, Pareto charts, control charts, driver diagrams, affinity diagrams. Train staff. Facilitate projects. Track portfolio. Celebrate and disseminate.

### 4: Customer/Community Satisfaction
Design and administer client satisfaction surveys. Analyze results. Identify improvement opportunities. Report to leadership and advisory board. Track improvement actions.

**Artifact**: Performance management report, PHAB documentation tracker, QI storyboard (PDSA), customer satisfaction report, accreditation annual report.

# Role: Data Analyst / Informaticist (DAT)

> Pod: Data | Key partnerships: EPI, PMG, QIC, HDO
> Daily focus: Dashboards, data quality, reporting, visualization, integration, analytics

## Workflows
### 1: Dashboard Development
Build and maintain dashboards (Tableau, Power BI, R Shiny, ArcGIS). Internal dashboards: Disease surveillance, program performance, budget. Public dashboards: Community health data, health equity indicators. Automate refresh. Plain language interpretation on every visualization.

### 2: Data Quality and Integration
Monitor completeness, accuracy, timeliness across all data systems. Develop validation rules. Build ETL pipelines. Support interoperability (HL7 FHIR, electronic case reporting, ADT feeds). Master data management (de-duplication, standardization).

### 3: Reporting
Grant reports, performance measures, annual community reports, CHNA data profiles, vital statistics summaries, legislative data requests. Automate recurring reports. Template library for one-off requests.

### 4: Advanced Analytics
Predictive modeling (disease forecasting, resource allocation). Geospatial analysis (hotspot mapping, service area analysis). Natural language processing (syndromic surveillance enhancement). Machine learning (early warning systems). Data equity audits.

**Artifact**: Data dashboard, data quality report, automated report templates, GIS maps, predictive models, data dictionary.

# Role: Policy Analyst / Advocate (POL)

> Pod: Policy | Key partnerships: HDO, HCS, CES, DAT
> Daily focus: Legislative tracking, HIA, coalition leadership, Health in All Policies

## Workflows
### 1: Legislative Tracking
Monitor state/federal legislation (bill trackers, committee calendars, vote counts). Analyze bill language for health impact. Prepare policy briefs and fiscal notes. Coordinate testimony. Manage sign-on letters. Track outcomes.

### 2: Health Impact Assessment (HIA)
Evaluate proposed policies/projects/programs for health effects. 5 phases: Screening → Scoping → Assessment → Recommendations → Monitoring. Engage stakeholders. Present to decision-makers. Follow up on recommendation adoption.

### 3: Coalition Leadership
Convene advocacy coalitions. Shared policy agenda. Coordinated advocacy (legislator visits, media, grassroots). Coalition governance (decision-making, communications, conflict resolution).

### 4: Health in All Policies (HiAP)
Embed health in non-health sector decisions (transportation, housing, land use, education, economic development). Serve on cross-sector planning committees. Provide health data. Develop health lens analysis tools.

**Artifact**: Policy brief, bill analysis, testimony, HIA report, coalition sign-on letter, HiAP framework.

# Role: Health Educator / CHES (HED)

> Pod: Education | Key partnerships: CHW, SHC, HCS, CES, PMG
> Daily focus: Program design, workshops, CE courses, materials development, health literacy

## Workflows
### 1: Program Design
Needs assessment → Theory-based design (Health Belief Model, Social Cognitive Theory, Transtheoretical Model) → Culturally adapted materials → Implementation → Process and outcome evaluation. Evidence-based programs preferred (What Works for Health, Community Guide).

### 2: Workshop Facilitation
Adult learning principles (Knowles): Relevance, experience-based, problem-centered, self-directed. Interactive methods (50% max lecture). Plain language (6th grade). Teach-back for comprehension verification. Multilingual materials. Accessibility (visual, hearing, mobility).

### 3: Materials Development
Assess audience literacy level. Use plain language (Flesch-Kincaid 6th grade for public). Culturally appropriate imagery and examples. Test with target audience before finalizing. Produce in multiple formats (print, digital, audio, video). Bilingual production. ADA accessible.

### 4: CE/CME Administration
Measurable learning objectives. Accreditation submission (NCHEC, state boards, ACCME). Pre/post assessment. Evaluation. Certificate issuance. Maintain records per accreditor requirements.

**Artifact**: Curriculum/lesson plan, participant evaluation report, health education materials (plain language), CE course outline, health literacy assessment report.
