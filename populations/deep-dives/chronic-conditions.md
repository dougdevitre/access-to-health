# Population Deep Dive: People Living with Chronic Conditions (CHR)

> Operational guidance for professionals serving this community

---

## Health Profile
- **Prevalence**: 60% of US adults have at least one chronic condition; 40% have two or more. Chronic diseases account for 90% of healthcare spending
- **Multimorbidity**: Co-occurring conditions (e.g., diabetes + depression + hypertension) are the norm, not the exception. Each additional condition compounds complexity
- **Polypharmacy**: People with multiple chronic conditions often take 5+ medications, increasing risk of adverse drug interactions and medication non-adherence
- **Quality of life**: Chronic pain, fatigue, functional limitations, and mental health burden are underrecognized and undertreated
- **Caregiver burden**: Family members provide the majority of chronic disease management support, often without training, compensation, or respite

## Structural Drivers
- **Fragmented care delivery**: Specialty silos mean no single provider manages the whole person. Care coordination is the exception, not the default
- **Cost barriers**: Out-of-pocket costs for medications, supplies, and specialist visits create ongoing financial strain, especially for insulin, inhalers, and biologics
- **Social determinants**: Food insecurity, housing instability, and transportation barriers make disease self-management nearly impossible
- **Health literacy demands**: Self-management requires understanding complex medication regimens, monitoring protocols, and dietary modifications
- **Workforce shortages**: Primary care and chronic disease management require time that current reimbursement models do not support

## Engagement Strategies
- **Primary care medical home**: Continuous, coordinated, team-based primary care is the evidence base for chronic disease management
- **Chronic Disease Self-Management Programs (CDSMP)**: Stanford model and similar evidence-based peer-led programs improve outcomes and self-efficacy
- **Peer support**: People living with the same conditions provide experiential knowledge, emotional support, and practical strategies
- **Community health workers**: Bridge clinical settings and daily life. Assist with navigation, self-management support, and social needs
- **Pharmacy-based interventions**: Pharmacists are accessible, trusted, and can provide medication therapy management and adherence support
- **Telehealth and remote monitoring**: Chronic conditions benefit from frequent low-intensity check-ins that telehealth enables
- **Integrated behavioral health**: Depression co-occurs with nearly every chronic condition and worsens outcomes. Screen and treat simultaneously

## Screening Considerations
- **Depression screening**: PHQ-2/PHQ-9 at every chronic disease visit. Depression is undertreated in chronic disease populations and directly worsens outcomes
- **Medication reconciliation**: Review all medications (including OTC and supplements) at every encounter to identify polypharmacy risks and adherence barriers
- **Functional assessment**: Screen for ADL/IADL limitations, falls risk, and caregiver needs, especially in people with multiple chronic conditions
