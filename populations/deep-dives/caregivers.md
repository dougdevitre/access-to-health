# Population Deep Dive: Caregivers (CAR)

> Operational guidance for supporting unpaid family caregivers

---

## Health Profile
- **Prevalence**: 53 million Americans provide unpaid care to an adult or child with special needs
- **Depression**: Caregiver depression rates 40-70% — 2-3x general population
- **Physical health**: Higher rates of chronic disease, immune suppression, and mortality
- **Financial stress**: Average caregiver spends $7,200/year out of pocket on caregiving expenses
- **Social isolation**: Caregiving duties limit social participation, employment, and self-care
- **Sleep disruption**: 50%+ of caregivers report poor sleep quality, especially for dementia caregivers
- **Delayed self-care**: Caregivers skip their own medical appointments, medications, and preventive care

## Structural Drivers
- **No paid family leave**: Only 27% of workers have paid family leave. FMLA is unpaid and excludes many workers
- **Long-term care costs**: Nursing home average >$90,000/year. Home care costs push caregiving onto families
- **Insurance gaps**: Respite care rarely covered by insurance. In-home support limited under Medicaid
- **Employer inflexibility**: Caregivers often reduce hours or leave jobs — losing income, benefits, and retirement savings
- **Gender disparity**: 60% of caregivers are women. Women of color are disproportionately represented

## Engagement Strategies
- **Identify caregivers**: They don't self-identify. Ask at clinical encounters: "Are you providing care for someone at home?"
- **Respite care**: Short-term relief is the #1 requested service. Area Agency on Aging, faith communities, volunteer programs
- **Support groups**: In-person and online. Alzheimer's Association, NAMI Family Support, caregiver-specific groups
- **Area Agency on Aging**: Comprehensive aging services including caregiver support, meals, transportation
- **Workplace accommodations**: Flexible schedules, telework, FMLA education, employee assistance programs
- **Self-care permission**: Caregivers need to hear: "Taking care of yourself is not selfish — it's necessary"
- **Advance care planning**: Help caregivers and care recipients discuss goals of care before crisis

## Screening Considerations
- Screen caregivers for depression (PHQ-9), burden (Zarit Burden Interview), and SDOH needs
- Caregivers often present with vague somatic complaints — dig deeper into stress and coping
- Ask about safety: "Do you ever feel so overwhelmed that you worry about losing your temper?"
- Refer to caregiver-specific resources, not generic mental health services
