# Population Deep Dive: Faith Communities (FAI)

> Operational guidance for partnering with faith-based organizations for health

---

## Health Profile
- **Trust advantage**: Faith communities are among the most trusted institutions, especially in Black, Latino, immigrant, and rural communities
- **Reach**: 65%+ of Americans are affiliated with a religious congregation — unmatched community access
- **Health ministries**: Many congregations already have health ministry programs (parish nursing, health fairs, support groups)
- **End-of-life**: Faith shapes decisions about advance directives, organ donation, palliative care, and grief
- **Mental health**: Clergy are often the first point of contact for people in emotional distress — before therapists or doctors

## Engagement Strategies
- **Partner with clergy first**: Pastors, imams, rabbis, and priests are gatekeepers. Build the relationship before asking for anything
- **Health ministries**: Support existing health ministry programs with training, materials, and screening resources
- **Parish nursing**: Nurses embedded in congregations — trusted bridge between health systems and community
- **Facility use**: Churches, mosques, temples are ideal locations for screening events, vaccination clinics, support groups
- **Respect theology**: Health messaging should complement, not contradict, spiritual beliefs
- **Cultural events**: Integrate health into existing events (Ramadan health fairs, Lenten wellness, VBS health activities)
- **Grief and bereavement**: Faith communities provide natural support — partner for overdose, suicide, and maternal death bereavement

## Screening Considerations
- **Setting**: Screen after services or at health ministry events. Private space within the facility
- **Framing**: "Your church/mosque/temple cares about your whole health — body, mind, and spirit"
- **Sensitive topics**: Approach mental health, substance use, and DV carefully — stigma may be higher in some faith communities
- **Respect boundaries**: Some congregants may decline services based on religious beliefs. Respect autonomy
