# Population Deep Dive: Children and Adolescents (CHD)

> Operational guidance for professionals serving this community

---

## Health Profile
- **Obesity**: 19.7% of children ages 2-19 have obesity; rates highest among Hispanic and Black youth. Associated with type 2 diabetes, asthma, and depression
- **Adverse childhood experiences (ACEs)**: Nearly half of US children have experienced at least one ACE. Cumulative ACEs linked to lifelong chronic disease and early mortality
- **Mental health**: 1 in 6 children ages 6-17 has a treatable mental health disorder; suicide is the second leading cause of death ages 10-14
- **Immunization gaps**: Disparities by geography and income. Undervaccinated children concentrated in rural and low-income communities
- **Lead exposure**: ~500,000 children ages 1-5 have elevated blood lead levels; disproportionately affects Black children in older housing

## Structural Drivers
- **School funding inequity**: Under-resourced schools lack nurses, counselors, and nutritious meal programs
- **Poverty**: Children are the poorest age group in the US. 1 in 6 children lives below the federal poverty level
- **Food environment**: Food deserts and targeted marketing of ultra-processed foods to children, especially children of color
- **Insurance churn**: Children cycle on and off Medicaid with family income changes, disrupting continuity of care
- **Housing instability**: Frequent moves disrupt schooling, healthcare, and social connections

## Engagement Strategies
- **Schools**: School-based health centers, school nurses, and after-school programs are the primary touchpoints
- **Pediatric primary care**: Well-child visits are structured screening opportunities (developmental, behavioral, nutritional)
- **Parents and caregivers**: Engage through parent-teacher organizations, WIC offices, and home visiting programs (e.g., Nurse-Family Partnership)
- **Youth-centered approaches**: Adolescents respond to peer education, youth advisory boards, and confidential services
- **Community recreation**: Parks, community centers, sports leagues provide access and trust
- **Child care settings**: Head Start and licensed child care centers reach children before school age
- **Digital engagement**: Adolescents are reachable through school-sanctioned digital platforms and text-based outreach

## Screening Considerations
- **Developmental screening**: Use validated tools (ASQ-3, M-CHAT-R/F) at recommended well-child intervals
- **ACEs screening**: Use age-appropriate tools; ensure follow-up resources are in place before screening. Never screen without a response plan
- **Confidentiality for adolescents**: Ensure minor consent laws are respected for reproductive health, mental health, and SUD services
