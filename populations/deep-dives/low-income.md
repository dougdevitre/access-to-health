# Population Deep Dive: People with Low Income (LIW)

> Operational guidance for professionals serving this community

---

## Health Profile
- **All-cause mortality**: Adults in the lowest income quintile die 10-15 years earlier than those in the highest quintile
- **Chronic disease burden**: Diabetes, heart disease, COPD, and stroke rates are significantly elevated. Poverty is the strongest predictor of poor health outcomes
- **Mental health**: Depression and anxiety rates are 2-3x higher. Toxic stress from financial insecurity has measurable physiological effects
- **Child health**: Children in poverty have higher rates of asthma, lead exposure, developmental delays, and food insecurity
- **Oral health**: Unmet dental needs are among the most prevalent health issues for people with low income. Medicaid dental coverage is limited in most states

## Structural Drivers
- **Benefits cliff**: Earning slightly above threshold results in loss of Medicaid, SNAP, housing assistance, and child care subsidies — creating a poverty trap
- **Wage stagnation**: Minimum wage has not kept pace with cost of living. Full-time minimum wage workers cannot afford a two-bedroom apartment in any US state
- **Housing cost burden**: Over 70% of extremely low-income renters spend more than half their income on housing, leaving little for food, medications, or transportation
- **Concentrated disadvantage**: Low-income communities have fewer grocery stores, parks, quality schools, and healthcare providers
- **Predatory systems**: Payday lending, medical debt collection, fines and fees, and credit reporting systems extract wealth from the poorest communities

## Engagement Strategies
- **Community action agencies**: Local anti-poverty organizations that provide energy assistance, weatherization, emergency services, and navigation
- **Benefits enrollment sites**: SNAP, TANF, Medicaid, WIC, and LIHEAP offices are high-traffic touchpoints for people with low income
- **Legal aid organizations**: Address systemic drivers — eviction defense, benefits appeals, medical debt, employment rights
- **FQHCs and free clinics**: Sliding-scale and no-cost care with integrated services
- **Schools**: Title I schools reach concentrated low-income populations. Free and reduced lunch programs are a proxy indicator
- **Faith-based organizations**: Food pantries, clothing closets, and emergency assistance through churches and congregations
- **Mobile outreach**: Bring services to neighborhoods with the greatest need rather than requiring travel to service centers

## Screening Considerations
- **Social determinants screening**: Use validated tools (PRAPARE, AHC HRSN) to identify food insecurity, housing instability, transportation barriers, and utility needs
- **Integrated referral systems**: Screening without referral capacity causes harm. Ensure closed-loop referral to community resources before initiating SDOH screening
- **Financial toxicity assessment**: For people managing chronic conditions, screen for medication cost barriers, medical debt, and insurance gaps
