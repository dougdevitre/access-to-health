# Population Deep Dive: People Who Are Uninsured (UNI)

> Operational guidance for professionals serving this community

---

## Health Profile
- **Delayed and forgone care**: Uninsured adults are 3-4x more likely to skip needed care, delay filling prescriptions, and avoid preventive services
- **Preventable hospitalization**: Without primary care access, people who are uninsured present to emergency departments with advanced, preventable conditions
- **Financial toxicity**: Medical debt is the leading cause of bankruptcy. Uninsured patients are billed at chargemaster rates — often 3-5x what insurers pay
- **Cancer outcomes**: Uninsured individuals are diagnosed at later stages and have higher mortality for nearly every cancer type
- **Chronic disease management**: Without consistent access to medications, monitoring, and specialist referrals, chronic conditions deteriorate

## Structural Drivers
- **Medicaid coverage gap**: In states that have not expanded Medicaid, adults earning below 100% FPL may be too poor for marketplace subsidies and too "wealthy" for Medicaid
- **Employer-sponsored insurance erosion**: Gig economy, part-time employment, and small businesses increasingly do not offer coverage
- **Immigration status exclusions**: Undocumented immigrants are ineligible for Medicaid and marketplace coverage in most states, regardless of ability to pay
- **Complexity of enrollment**: Marketplace enrollment requires digital literacy, documentation, and annual renewal. Administrative burden functions as a barrier
- **Cost of coverage**: Even with subsidies, deductibles and copays can make insurance functionally unusable for people with low income

## Engagement Strategies
- **Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs)**: Mandated to serve all patients regardless of insurance status on a sliding fee scale
- **Marketplace navigators and certified application counselors**: Provide free enrollment assistance during and outside open enrollment periods
- **Hospital charity care and financial assistance**: Nonprofit hospitals are required to have financial assistance policies. Help patients apply
- **Free and charitable clinics**: Over 1,400 free clinics nationally. Remote Area Medical and similar organizations provide episodic care
- **Prescription assistance programs**: Manufacturer patient assistance programs, 340B drug pricing, and GoodRx-type discount tools
- **Emergency Medicaid**: Available regardless of immigration status for emergency conditions. Ensure billing departments are aware
- **Presumptive eligibility**: Many states allow immediate temporary Medicaid enrollment at hospitals and qualified entities

## Screening Considerations
- **Insurance status screening**: Ask without judgment. Many people do not know they are eligible for coverage
- **Financial assistance screening**: Proactively screen for charity care eligibility at every encounter. Do not wait for patients to ask
- **Coverage transitions**: Screen for life events (job loss, aging out, divorce, pregnancy) that create enrollment opportunities
