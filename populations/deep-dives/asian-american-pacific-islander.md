# Population Deep Dive: Asian American and Pacific Islander Communities (API)

> Operational guidance for professionals serving this community

---

## Health Profile
- **Hepatitis B**: Asian Americans account for over half of chronic hepatitis B cases in the US despite being ~6% of the population. Many are undiagnosed
- **Cancer screening gaps**: Cervical and colorectal cancer screening rates are among the lowest of any racial group. Liver and stomach cancer incidence elevated
- **Disaggregation reveals disparity**: Aggregated data masks vast differences — Native Hawaiians and Pacific Islanders have diabetes, obesity, and mortality rates far exceeding the Asian American average
- **Mental health underutilization**: Asian Americans are the least likely racial group to seek mental health services. Cultural stigma and model minority pressure suppress help-seeking
- **Cardiovascular risk**: South Asian Americans have elevated cardiovascular disease risk at lower BMI thresholds than standard screening guidelines recognize

## Structural Drivers
- **Model minority myth**: Aggregated statistics mask poverty, language barriers, and health disparities among Southeast Asian, Pacific Islander, and other subgroups
- **Language diversity**: Over 100 languages spoken. Limited English proficiency rates are among the highest of any racial group, yet interpreter access remains inadequate
- **Immigration stress**: Many community members carry refugee trauma (Southeast Asian), documentation anxiety, or public charge fears that deter service use
- **Data aggregation**: Federal data collection that lumps 50+ ethnic groups into one category makes disparities invisible and defunds targeted interventions
- **Anti-Asian discrimination**: Hate crimes and discrimination increased significantly during COVID-19, compounding mental health burden and institutional mistrust

## Engagement Strategies
- **Ethnic community organizations**: Chinese, Vietnamese, Korean, Filipino, Samoan, and other ethnic-specific organizations are trusted entry points
- **Religious and cultural institutions**: Temples, churches, mosques, and cultural centers serve as community anchors
- **Cultural brokers and bilingual staff**: Language-concordant care dramatically improves access and outcomes
- **Disaggregate outreach**: Tailor materials and strategies to specific ethnic communities — one-size-fits-all approaches fail
- **Community health fairs**: Screening events at cultural festivals and community gatherings reduce stigma
- **Intergenerational approaches**: Engage elders through family networks; engage youth through schools and social media
- **Pacific Islander-specific strategies**: Partner with Native Hawaiian and Pacific Islander organizations separately — their needs and history are distinct

## Screening Considerations
- **Hepatitis B screening**: Universal screening for people born in high-prevalence countries. Ensure linkage to treatment, not just diagnosis
- **Adjusted BMI thresholds**: WHO recommends lower BMI cutoffs for Asian populations (overweight at 23, obese at 27.5) for cardiovascular and diabetes risk
- **Culturally sensitive mental health screening**: Somatic presentations of depression and anxiety are common. Use culturally validated instruments where available
