# Population Deep Dive: People Living with Mental Health Conditions (MHC)

> Operational guidance for professionals serving this community

---

## Health Profile
- **Premature mortality**: People with serious mental illness die 15-20 years earlier than the general population, primarily from cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and respiratory illness — not suicide
- **Co-occurring substance use**: Roughly 50% of people with serious mental illness also experience a substance use disorder. Integrated treatment is the standard of care
- **Physical health neglect**: Metabolic syndrome is common, partly driven by antipsychotic medications and partly by exclusion from routine preventive care
- **Suicide**: Over 90% of people who die by suicide had a diagnosable mental health condition. Access to lethal means is the strongest modifiable risk factor
- **Treatment gaps**: Over 50% of adults with mental illness receive no treatment in a given year. For Black and Latino adults, that gap exceeds 60%

## Structural Drivers
- **Workforce shortage**: The US has roughly half the mental health providers it needs. Rural and low-income areas are most affected
- **Criminalization**: Jails and prisons have become the largest mental health facilities in most states. Arrest replaces treatment
- **Insurance discrimination**: Despite parity laws, insurers impose higher cost-sharing, narrower networks, and prior authorization requirements for mental health care
- **Stigma**: Structural stigma (housing discrimination, employment exclusion) and internalized stigma delay help-seeking by an average of 11 years from symptom onset
- **Fragmented systems**: Mental health, substance use, physical health, and social services operate in separate systems with separate funding, separate records, and separate providers

## Engagement Strategies
- **988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline**: National crisis number with call, text, and chat. Connects to local crisis centers
- **NAMI (National Alliance on Mental Illness)**: Peer and family education, support groups, and advocacy in every state
- **Certified peer specialists**: People with lived experience of mental health conditions providing support, navigation, and hope. Evidence-based workforce
- **Integrated care models**: Collaborative care (CoCM) and behavioral health integration in primary care settings improve access and outcomes
- **Crisis alternatives**: Crisis stabilization units, mobile crisis teams, and crisis respite centers as alternatives to emergency departments and jails
- **Supported employment and housing**: Individual Placement and Support (IPS) and Housing First models address the social determinants most relevant to recovery
- **Warm handoffs**: Never give a person in crisis a phone number and wish them well. Walk them to the next service, make the call together, or stay until connection is made

## Screening Considerations
- **Universal screening in primary care**: PHQ-2/PHQ-9 for depression, GAD-2/GAD-7 for anxiety. Most mental health conditions present first in primary care
- **Suicide risk assessment**: Use structured tools (Columbia Protocol, ASQ). Ask directly about suicidal thoughts — asking does not increase risk
- **Trauma screening**: ACEs, PCL-5 for PTSD. Trauma history is nearly universal in people with serious mental illness. Screen with care and only when response resources are available
