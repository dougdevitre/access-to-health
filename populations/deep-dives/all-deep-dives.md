# Population Deep Dives — 9 Populations

> Operational guidance for professionals serving specific communities
> Not health education sheets — structural analysis, cultural context, and actionable strategies

## Hispanic / Latino (HIS)

### Health Profile
- **Diabetes**: 1.7x higher prevalence than white adults. Higher rates of uncontrolled diabetes due to access barriers
- **Cervical cancer**: Higher incidence due to HPV screening gaps
- **Occupational injuries**: Overrepresented in agriculture, construction, and food processing — high-hazard industries
- **Tuberculosis**: Higher rates, particularly among foreign-born populations
- **Maternal health**: Lower prenatal care access despite better-than-expected birth outcomes in some measures (Hispanic paradox — likely driven by selection and cultural protective factors)
- **Uninsured rate**: Highest of any racial/ethnic group (~18%). Immigration status fear suppresses enrollment
- **Mental health**: Comparable prevalence but significantly lower treatment rates. "Nervios" and "susto" may be presenting complaints for depression/anxiety/PTSD

### Root Causes — Structural, Not Individual
1. **Immigration enforcement fear**: Suppresses healthcare utilization, benefit enrollment, and reporting of abuse or workplace violations — even among citizens and legal residents
2. **Language barriers**: 25 million Americans have limited English proficiency. Health information is often English-only
3. **Occupational exploitation**: Dangerous jobs, low wages, no benefits, retaliation for complaints
4. **Educational inequity**: Language barriers limit school success and health literacy
5. **Housing instability**: Overcrowding, substandard housing, fear of reporting violations
6. **Political disenfranchisement**: Non-citizen residents cannot vote, reducing political power to change health-harming policies

### Cultural Considerations
- **Familismo**: Family is central. Include family in health decisions when the client invites it. Decisions are often collective
- **Personalismo**: Warm, personal relationships > institutional efficiency. Take time for greeting and rapport
- **Respeto**: Deep respect for elders and authority. Use formal address (usted) until invited otherwise
- **Confianza**: Trust built through consistent, personal relationships — not institutions. Promotoras are the gold standard
- **Machismo/Marianismo**: Traditional gender roles may affect who seeks care and how. Don't assume — ask
- **Susto/Nervios**: Folk illness concepts that may present as anxiety, depression, PTSD. Don't dismiss — integrate with medical assessment
- See `bilingual/spanish-layer.md` for screening scripts, cultural concepts, and safe spaces protocol

### Engagement Strategies
- **Promotoras de salud**: Community health workers from the community — trusted, culturally connected, bilingual
- **Catholic parishes**: Central institution in many Latino communities
- **Tiendas, laundromats, soccer fields**: Where the community gathers
- **Spanish-language media**: Radio (highest reach), TV, social media
- **Mexican consulates (Ventanillas de Salud)**: Health screening at consular offices
- **Always clarify**: "This service does not ask about immigration status. We do not share information with immigration authorities."

## Pregnant and Postpartum (PRG)

### Health Profile
- **Maternal mortality**: ~32/100,000 live births (US) — highest among wealthy nations. 80% preventable
- **Racial disparity**: Black women 3-4x higher mortality. Driven by structural racism, provider bias, weathering
- **Leading causes of maternal death**: Hemorrhage, hypertension/preeclampsia, cardiomyopathy, infection, mental health conditions (including suicide and overdose)
- **Perinatal depression**: Affects 15-20% of pregnant/postpartum persons. Underscreened and undertreated
- **Substance use in pregnancy**: 5-8% report alcohol, tobacco, or drug use. Stigma prevents disclosure and treatment
- **Preterm birth**: ~10% overall, ~14% Black. Leading cause of infant mortality
- **Gestational diabetes**: 6-9% of pregnancies. Risk factor for Type 2 diabetes

### Screening Adaptations
- Screen for depression at **every** prenatal AND postpartum visit (Edinburgh, PHQ-9)
- Screen for IPV in a **private** setting — never with partner present
- Screen for substance use using validated tool (4Ps, NIDA) — nonjudgmental, treatment not punishment
- Ask about discrimination: "Have you ever felt a doctor or nurse treated you unfairly because of your race?"
- SDOH screening: Housing, food, transportation are critical — address before health education
- Assess birth plan preferences: Many Black women want to avoid unnecessary interventions given medical exploitation history

### Engagement Strategies
- **Doulas**: Community-based doula programs reduce preterm birth by 30% and improve satisfaction
- **Centering Pregnancy**: Group prenatal care model — improves outcomes and social support
- **Home visiting**: NFP, PAT, HFA — evidence-based programs for high-risk pregnancies
- **WIC**: Nutrition support, breastfeeding education, referral hub
- **Postpartum care**: Emphasize that the 6-week visit is for **the parent's** health, not just the baby
- **Peer support**: Parent support groups, breastfeeding circles, postpartum wellness checks

### Critical Safety Rules
- Postpartum warning signs (teach BEFORE delivery): Severe headache, vision changes, swelling, heavy bleeding, fever, chest pain, difficulty breathing, thoughts of self-harm
- "If something doesn't feel right, trust your instincts and go to the ER. You know your body."
- Postpartum extends to 1 year — mental health and cardiac causes peak at 6-12 months post-delivery

## People Experiencing Homelessness (HML)

### Health Profile
- **Life expectancy**: 12+ years shorter than general population. Average age of death ~50
- **Mental illness**: 30-40% — often untreated, compounding homelessness
- **Substance use disorders**: 30-40% — often intertwined with trauma and mental illness
- **Infectious disease**: TB, hepatitis, HIV, skin infections, respiratory infections — crowded shelters, unsanitary conditions
- **Chronic disease**: Hypertension, diabetes, COPD — unmanaged without consistent care
- **Dental disease**: Near-universal unmet dental needs
- **Injuries**: Falls, assault, frostbite, heat illness, pedestrian injury
- **Mortality**: Overdose, suicide, homicide, exposure, and treatable medical conditions

### Root Causes — Systems, Not Choices
1. **Housing cost**: Wages haven't kept pace with housing costs. Median renter can't afford a 2BR in any US state at minimum wage
2. **Eviction**: A single eviction creates a cascade — job loss, school disruption, shelter entry, chronic homelessness
3. **Mental illness/SUD**: Deinstitutionalization without community investment. Treatment inaccessible
4. **Domestic violence**: Leading cause of homelessness for women and children
5. **Foster care aging out**: Youth aging out at 18 with no support — 25% experience homelessness within 4 years
6. **Incarceration**: Housing discrimination against people with criminal records
7. **Medical debt**: Leading cause of bankruptcy

### Engagement Strategies
- **Meet where they are**: Street outreach, shelters, day centers, encampments, libraries, syringe programs
- **Low barrier**: No ID required. No sobriety required. No appointments required
- **Housing First**: The evidence says: house people first, then address health. Not the reverse
- **Harm reduction**: Meet people where they are with substance use. Naloxone, fentanyl test strips, wound care
- **Trauma-informed everything**: Most people experiencing homelessness have experienced significant trauma
- **Practical needs first**: Food, water, clothing, hygiene, safety — before health education
- **Document nothing**: Don't require documentation the person doesn't have. Build trust first

## People Who Use Drugs (PWUD)

### Health Profile
- **Overdose**: Leading cause of death ages 18-45. Fentanyl dominates the illicit supply
- **HIV/HCV**: Injection drug use remains a major transmission route. 70-80% of PWID have HCV
- **Endocarditis**: Injection-related bacterial heart valve infection — often fatal without surgery
- **Skin and soft tissue infections**: Abscesses, cellulitis, wound botulism, necrotizing fasciitis
- **Dental disease**: Severe — both from drug effects and access barriers
- **Incarceration**: Drug criminalization creates cycling through jails. Overdose risk skyrockets at release
- **Stigma**: The single largest barrier to care. Stigma kills more than drugs do

### Harm Reduction Framework
**Harm reduction is evidence-based healthcare, not enabling.** It saves lives.

| Intervention | Evidence |
|-------------|----------|
| **Naloxone distribution** | Reverses opioid overdose. No abuse potential. Available without prescription in most states |
| **Syringe service programs** | Reduce HIV/HCV by 50%+. Do NOT increase drug use. Serve as entry point for treatment |
| **Fentanyl test strips** | Allow people to test their supply. Reduce overdose deaths. Low-cost, high-impact |
| **Medications for OUD** | Methadone, buprenorphine, naltrexone. Reduce overdose death by 50-75%. First-line treatment |
| **Supervised consumption** | Reduce overdose death. Reduce public injection. Increase treatment entry. Legal in some jurisdictions |
| **Drug checking** | Identify adulterants (fentanyl, xylazine, nitazenes). Inform harm reduction response |
| **Wound care** | Basic wound care at syringe programs prevents hospital admissions |

### Language Rules
- **Say**: "Person who uses drugs" — NOT "addict," "junkie," "abuser," "substance abuser"
- **Say**: "Positive drug test" — NOT "dirty"
- **Say**: "Use" or "misuse" — NOT "abuse"
- **Say**: "Medication for opioid use disorder" — NOT "replacement" or "substitution"
- **Say**: "Has a substance use disorder" — NOT "is an addict"

### Engagement
- Syringe service programs, mobile outreach, peer recovery specialists with lived experience
- **Never** require abstinence as a condition of receiving healthcare or social services
- **Never** discharge someone from care for positive drug tests
- Good Samaritan laws: Calling 911 for an overdose is protected

## Immigrant and Refugee (IMM)

### Health Profile
- **Screening needs at arrival**: TB (IGRA), hepatitis B, parasites, lead (children), immunization gaps, dental, vision, mental health (RHS-15)
- **Trauma/PTSD**: Refugees and asylum seekers have very high rates. Torture survivors need specialized care
- **Chronic disease**: May be undiagnosed due to limited prior healthcare access
- **Maternal health**: Higher rates of low birth weight in some groups. Cultural practices around pregnancy/birth
- **Dental**: Often extensive unmet needs
- **Infectious disease**: Varies by country of origin — TB, malaria, hepatitis, parasites

### Access Barriers
1. **Immigration status fear**: Even eligible immigrants avoid services. Public charge rule confusion
2. **Language**: Interpreter access is legally required but often unavailable
3. **Cultural mismatch**: Western medical model may conflict with traditional health beliefs
4. **Credential non-recognition**: Healthcare professionals from other countries can't practice
5. **Transportation**: Unfamiliarity with systems, lack of driver's license, rural isolation
6. **Insurance**: Eligibility varies by status, duration, and state

### Engagement
- **Cultural brokers**: People from the community who bridge cultures and systems
- **Ethnic community organizations**: Somali Bantu Association, Bosnian community center, etc.
- **Resettlement agencies**: International Rescue Committee, Catholic Charities, USCRI
- **Faith communities**: Mosques, temples, churches that serve specific ethnic groups
- **ESL classrooms**: Health education integrated with English learning
- Workflow: See `workflows/cross-role-workflows-expanded.md` Workflow 6

## LGBTQ+ (LGBT)

### Health Profile
- **Mental health**: 2-3x higher rates of depression and anxiety. 4x suicide attempt rate in youth
- **HIV**: MSM account for ~70% of new HIV diagnoses. Transgender women at very high risk
- **Substance use**: Higher rates of tobacco, alcohol, and drug use — driven by minority stress
- **Cancer**: Elevated risk for certain cancers (anal, cervical in trans men, breast in trans women)
- **Violence**: Higher rates of IPV, hate crimes, and sexual assault
- **Provider bias**: 8% of LGB and 29% of transgender people report being refused care
- **Youth**: Family rejection is the #1 predictor of negative health outcomes for LGBTQ+ youth

### Clinical Considerations
- **Intake forms**: Include sexual orientation and gender identity (SOGI) fields. Ask preferred name and pronouns
- **Chosen name**: Use chosen name in all interactions. Legal name only for insurance/billing when required
- **Affirming language**: "Partner" not "husband/wife" until told. "Assigned male/female at birth" not "biological"
- **Trans-specific care**: Hormone therapy, surgical referrals, cancer screening based on anatomy not gender marker
- **PrEP**: Pre-exposure prophylaxis for HIV prevention. Discuss with all MSM and transgender patients at risk
- **Do NOT assume**: Sexual orientation and gender identity are separate from each other and from behavior

### Engagement
- **LGBTQ+ community centers**: Primary trusted institutions
- **Pride organizations**: Events, social networks, community building
- **GSAs (Gender-Sexuality Alliances)**: In schools — critical for youth
- **Affirming providers**: Maintain a directory of LGBTQ+-affirming healthcare providers
- **Online communities**: Many LGBTQ+ people, especially in rural areas, find community online first
- **Ballroom/house culture**: Important in Black and Latino LGBTQ+ communities

## Older Adults (OAD)

### Health Profile
- **Falls**: #1 cause of injury death in 65+. 1 in 4 older adults falls each year
- **Dementia**: 10% of 65+, 33% of 85+. Caregiver burden is enormous
- **Polypharmacy**: Average 65+ person takes 5+ medications. Drug interactions and adverse effects common
- **Depression**: 7% prevalence, higher in institutionalized. Often undiagnosed ("just part of aging" — it's not)
- **Social isolation**: 25% of 65+ are socially isolated. Isolation is as deadly as smoking 15 cigarettes/day
- **Elder abuse**: 1 in 10 older adults experiences abuse (physical, emotional, financial, neglect)
- **Chronic disease burden**: 80%+ have at least one chronic condition. 68% have two or more

### Screening
- **Falls**: Stay Independent questionnaire, Timed Up and Go
- **Depression**: Geriatric Depression Scale (GDS) — designed for older adults
- **Cognitive function**: Mini-Cog (clock drawing + 3-word recall)
- **Medication review**: Annual comprehensive review by pharmacist
- **SDOH**: Social isolation, food access, transportation, financial strain, safety
- **Advance care planning**: Goals of care conversation — not a one-time event

### Engagement
- **Senior centers**: Hub for services, social connection, health programming
- **Meals on Wheels**: Home-delivered meals + daily check-in + socialization
- **Area Agency on Aging**: Comprehensive aging services network
- **Faith communities**: Churches are central social networks for many older adults
- **Medicare counseling (SHIP)**: Insurance navigation
- **Pharmacies**: Trusted, accessible, frequent touchpoint

## Justice-Involved / Reentry (JUS)

### Health Profile
- **Mental illness**: 50%+ — often untreated during and after incarceration
- **Substance use disorders**: 65%+ — treatment access extremely limited in custody
- **Chronic disease**: Hypertension, diabetes, asthma, hepatitis — poorly managed during incarceration
- **Infectious disease**: HIV, HCV, TB, STI — concentrated by overcrowding and lack of testing
- **Dental disease**: Nearly universal unmet dental needs
- **Traumatic brain injury**: 25-87% depending on population — often undiagnosed
- **Overdose**: Risk of death from overdose is **129x higher** in the 2 weeks after release from incarceration

### Critical Reentry Needs (First 72 Hours)
1. **Medications**: 30-day supply at release (many facilities release with 0-3 days)
2. **Medicaid enrollment**: Suspend during incarceration, reactivate at release (not terminate)
3. **SUD treatment**: MAT continuity — do NOT stop buprenorphine or methadone at release
4. **Mental health**: Appointment within 7 days of release
5. **Housing**: Halfway house, transitional housing, or family placement (many face housing restrictions)
6. **ID**: Help obtaining state ID, Social Security card, birth certificate
7. **Naloxone**: Provide at release with training — overdose risk peaks immediately

### Engagement
- **Reentry programs**: Workforce development, case management, mentoring
- **Peer specialists with lived experience**: People who have been incarcerated themselves
- **Legal aid**: Expungement, driver's license reinstatement, employment rights
- **Family**: Reconnection support, family counseling, co-parenting
- **Parole/probation**: Coordinate care (with consent) to avoid re-incarceration for health issues
- **Never**: Make health services contingent on criminal justice compliance. Healthcare is a right

## Rural Communities (RUR)

### Health Profile
- **Provider shortage**: 65% of Health Professional Shortage Areas are rural. Fewer specialists, longer waits
- **Hospital closures**: 130+ rural hospitals closed since 2010. More at risk
- **Higher mortality**: Rural residents have higher death rates for heart disease, cancer, unintentional injury, stroke, and COPD
- **Substance use**: Methamphetamine crisis. Opioid overdose rates rising. Less treatment access
- **Suicide**: Rural rates 2x urban. Firearm access + fewer mental health providers + cultural stigma
- **Obesity/diabetes**: Higher rates — food deserts, fewer recreational facilities, occupational patterns
- **Agricultural health**: Pesticide exposure, injuries, respiratory disease, heat illness, mental health (farm stress)

### Access Barriers
1. **Distance**: Average rural resident lives 10+ miles from a hospital. Specialty care may be 60+ miles
2. **Transportation**: Limited public transit. Car dependence. Cost of travel
3. **Broadband**: 25% of rural residents lack broadband. Limits telehealth, health information, appointment scheduling
4. **Workforce**: Providers leave rural areas for higher pay and urban amenities
5. **Stigma**: Small communities — "everyone knows everyone." Seeking mental health or SUD treatment = public knowledge
6. **Economics**: Lower wages, fewer benefits, agricultural income volatility

### Engagement
- **Telehealth**: Expand dramatically. Audio-only for those without broadband/smartphones
- **Mobile clinics**: Bring services to the community (screening, immunization, dental)
- **Churches**: Central institution in rural life. Health ministry partnerships
- **Farm Bureau / Extension Service**: Trusted agricultural organizations. AgriSafe Network for farmer health
- **Schools**: Often the community center. School-based health services
- **Local pharmacies**: Trusted, accessible, often the only healthcare touchpoint
- **Volunteer organizations**: Fire departments, Lions/Rotary, 4-H — strong civic networks
- **OATS Transit**: Rural transportation network (Missouri)
