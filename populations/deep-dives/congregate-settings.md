# Population Deep Dive: Congregate Settings (CON)

> Operational guidance for serving people in jails, shelters, group homes, and nursing facilities

---

## Health Profile
- **Infectious disease**: TB, COVID-19, influenza, scabies, MRSA — spread rapidly in close quarters
- **Mental illness**: 50-75% prevalence in jails. Often untreated or undertreated in facility
- **Substance use disorders**: 65%+ in jails. Withdrawal management is a medical emergency
- **Chronic disease**: Hypertension, diabetes, asthma, HIV/HCV — poorly managed during stays
- **Dental disease**: Near-universal unmet needs across all congregate settings
- **Violence and trauma**: Assault, sexual violence, and re-traumatization common in jails and some shelters

## Setting-Specific Considerations

### Jails and Prisons
- Intake screening within 24 hours (medical, mental health, SUD, suicide risk)
- Medication continuity — do NOT stop psychiatric meds or MAT at intake
- Discharge planning starts at intake: Medicaid, medications, appointments, housing, ID
- Overdose risk is 129x higher in first 2 weeks post-release — provide naloxone at discharge

### Shelters
- Low-barrier approach: no sobriety requirements, no ID requirements
- Screen for TB (symptom screen minimum, testing if available)
- Mental health and SUD assessment — connect to services, don't require treatment
- Trauma-informed environment: predictable routines, respect for privacy and autonomy

### Group Homes / Assisted Living
- Medication management and monitoring
- Fall prevention for older adult residents
- Abuse and neglect screening (staff and resident)
- Infection control protocols

### Nursing Facilities
- Infection prevention and control (hand hygiene, isolation, vaccination)
- Advance care planning and goals of care conversations
- Depression screening (GDS) — depression is underdiagnosed in nursing homes
- Social isolation interventions — activities, family engagement, technology

## Engagement Strategies
- **Work WITH facility staff**, not around them — they know the population
- **Mobile health services**: Bring screening, vaccination, dental to the facility
- **Discharge/transition planning**: Health department role is ensuring continuity of care at exit
- **Staff training**: Trauma-informed care, mental health first aid, naloxone administration
- **Advocacy**: Minimum health standards, adequate staffing, access to medications
