# Population Deep Dive: Native American and Alaska Native Communities (NAT)

> Operational guidance for professionals serving this community

---

## Health Profile
- **Diabetes**: Native Americans have the highest age-adjusted diabetes prevalence of any racial group in the US — 2-3x the national average
- **Substance use and suicide**: Alcohol-related mortality is 5x higher than national average. Suicide rates are 1.5x higher, with youth disproportionately affected
- **Life expectancy**: 5.5 years shorter than the US average. Leading causes: heart disease, cancer, unintentional injuries, diabetes, liver disease
- **Maternal and infant health**: Infant mortality rates roughly 1.5x the national average. Significant prenatal care access gaps on reservations
- **Historical and intergenerational trauma**: Boarding schools, forced relocations, and genocide have produced measurable epigenetic and psychological effects across generations

## Structural Drivers
- **Chronic underfunding of IHS**: Indian Health Service is funded at roughly 50% of need. Per-capita spending is a fraction of federal employee or Medicaid spending
- **Treaty obligations unmet**: Healthcare is a legal obligation under treaties, not a benefit. The federal government has consistently failed to fulfill this obligation
- **Geographic isolation**: Many reservations are in remote areas with few providers, limited broadband, and long distances to specialty care
- **Jurisdictional complexity**: Overlapping tribal, state, and federal authority creates gaps in service delivery and data systems
- **Environmental injustice**: Uranium mining, water contamination, and climate change disproportionately affect tribal lands

## Engagement Strategies
- **Respect sovereignty**: Tribal nations are sovereign governments. Engage tribal leadership and follow tribal IRB and data governance protocols
- **Tribal health programs**: Tribally-operated health programs (under P.L. 93-638 self-determination contracts) are the trusted delivery system
- **Community Health Representatives (CHRs)**: Tribal community health workers with deep cultural knowledge and community trust
- **Cultural leaders and elders**: Ceremony, language, and traditional healing are health interventions. Support integration with clinical care
- **Urban Indian Health Programs**: 60-70% of Native Americans live in urban areas. Urban Indian health organizations bridge the gap
- **Data sovereignty**: Tribes own their data. Never publish or share tribal health data without explicit tribal consent and approval

## Screening Considerations
- **Culturally grounded tools**: Use screening tools validated for Native communities where available. Standard tools may not capture culturally specific expressions of distress
- **Trauma-informed, historically aware**: Screening must acknowledge historical trauma. Clinical encounters can trigger institutional mistrust rooted in real experiences
- **Diabetes and metabolic screening**: Begin early and screen consistently given elevated population risk
