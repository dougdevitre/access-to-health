# Population Deep Dive: College / University Students (COL)

> Operational guidance for serving college and university student populations

---

## Health Profile
- **Mental health crisis**: 60%+ of college students report significant anxiety or depression. Suicide is the 2nd leading cause of death
- **Substance use**: Binge drinking affects ~33% of students. Cannabis, stimulant misuse, and vaping are prevalent
- **Sexual health**: High STI rates. Inconsistent condom use. Sexual assault affects ~25% of women and ~7% of men
- **Food insecurity**: 30-50% of students at public universities experience food insecurity
- **Sleep deprivation**: Average student gets <7 hours. Impacts academic performance, mental health, and immune function
- **Eating disorders**: Prevalence 2-3x general population, especially among women, LGBTQ+ students, and athletes

## Structural Drivers
- **Cost of attendance**: Tuition, housing, and food costs create financial stress and food insecurity
- **Transition stress**: First time away from family support networks. Identity development. Academic pressure
- **Insurance gaps**: Students aging off parents' insurance at 26. International students often underinsured
- **Stigma**: Campus culture may normalize excessive drinking and discourage help-seeking for mental health

## Engagement Strategies
- **Campus health centers**: Primary touchpoint — integrate SDOH screening into visits
- **Counseling centers**: Often overwhelmed — supplement with peer counseling, group therapy, crisis text line
- **Residence halls**: RA training on mental health first aid, substance use, and sexual assault response
- **Peer education**: Students trust peers more than administrators. Train peer health educators
- **Greek life**: Fraternities and sororities have both risk factors and reach — partner for harm reduction
- **Student organizations**: Cultural orgs, LGBTQ+ groups, veteran student orgs as engagement channels
- **Food pantries**: Campus food pantries and SNAP enrollment assistance

## Screening Considerations
- Use **Quick Screen** at health center visits — low burden, catches critical needs
- **PHQ-2/PHQ-9** for depression at every visit (normalize: "we ask everyone")
- **AUDIT-C** for alcohol — brief intervention for positive screens
- Ask about food security, housing stability, and financial stress
