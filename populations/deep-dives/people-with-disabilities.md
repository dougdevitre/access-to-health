# Population Deep Dive: People with Disabilities (DIS)

> Operational guidance for professionals serving this community

---

## Health Profile
- **Secondary conditions**: People with disabilities experience higher rates of obesity, depression, chronic pain, and cardiovascular disease — often due to inaccessible health promotion, not the disability itself
- **Preventive care gaps**: Adults with disabilities are significantly less likely to receive routine cancer screenings, dental care, and wellness visits
- **Mental health**: Depression rates 2-3x higher than general population. Social isolation, discrimination, and chronic pain are key drivers
- **Abuse and violence**: People with disabilities experience violence at rates 2-3x higher than people without disabilities, including from caregivers and institutional staff
- **Premature mortality**: People with intellectual and developmental disabilities die 16-20 years earlier than the general population, largely from preventable causes

## Structural Drivers
- **Physical inaccessibility**: Medical equipment (exam tables, scales, imaging) remains inaccessible in most clinical settings despite ADA requirements
- **Provider knowledge gaps**: Most clinicians receive minimal disability-specific training; diagnostic overshadowing attributes symptoms to the disability rather than investigating new conditions
- **Employment discrimination**: Unemployment rate for people with disabilities is roughly double the general rate, limiting income and employer-sponsored insurance
- **Benefits cliff**: Earning above SSI/SSDI thresholds can trigger loss of Medicaid, creating a poverty trap
- **Institutionalization bias**: Olmstead decision (1999) mandates community integration, yet waitlists for home and community-based services persist in every state

## Engagement Strategies
- **Centers for Independent Living (CILs)**: Peer-run organizations in every state providing advocacy, skills training, and service coordination
- **Disability rights organizations**: Trusted community voices. Partner, do not paternalize
- **Accessible communication**: Provide materials in plain language, large print, Braille, ASL interpretation, and screen-reader compatible formats
- **Nothing About Us Without Us**: Include people with disabilities in program design, hiring, and evaluation
- **Home-based services**: For people with mobility limitations, bring services to them — telehealth, mobile clinics, home visits
- **Self-advocacy organizations**: People First, ASAN, and similar groups provide trusted peer networks

## Screening Considerations
- **Adapted screening tools**: Standard tools may need modification for people with intellectual disabilities or communication differences. Use supported decision-making, not surrogate decision-making by default
- **Comprehensive health assessments**: Screen for secondary conditions proactively. Do not attribute new symptoms to the existing disability
- **Abuse screening**: Screen in private, away from caregivers. Use accessible reporting mechanisms
