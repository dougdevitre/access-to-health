# Population Deep Dive: Pregnant and Postpartum (PRG)

> Operational guidance for professionals serving this community
> Not a health education sheet — structural analysis, cultural context, and actionable strategies

---

## Health Profile
- **Maternal mortality**: ~32/100,000 live births (US) — highest among wealthy nations. 80% preventable
- **Racial disparity**: Black women 3-4x higher mortality. Driven by structural racism, provider bias, weathering
- **Leading causes of maternal death**: Hemorrhage, hypertension/preeclampsia, cardiomyopathy, infection, mental health conditions (including suicide and overdose)
- **Perinatal depression**: Affects 15-20% of pregnant/postpartum persons. Underscreened and undertreated
- **Substance use in pregnancy**: 5-8% report alcohol, tobacco, or drug use. Stigma prevents disclosure and treatment
- **Preterm birth**: ~10% overall, ~14% Black. Leading cause of infant mortality
- **Gestational diabetes**: 6-9% of pregnancies. Risk factor for Type 2 diabetes

## Screening Adaptations
- Screen for depression at **every** prenatal AND postpartum visit (Edinburgh, PHQ-9)
- Screen for IPV in a **private** setting — never with partner present
- Screen for substance use using validated tool (4Ps, NIDA) — nonjudgmental, treatment not punishment
- Ask about discrimination: "Have you ever felt a doctor or nurse treated you unfairly because of your race?"
- SDOH screening: Housing, food, transportation are critical — address before health education
- Assess birth plan preferences: Many Black women want to avoid unnecessary interventions given medical exploitation history

## Engagement Strategies
- **Doulas**: Community-based doula programs reduce preterm birth by 30% and improve satisfaction
- **Centering Pregnancy**: Group prenatal care model — improves outcomes and social support
- **Home visiting**: NFP, PAT, HFA — evidence-based programs for high-risk pregnancies
- **WIC**: Nutrition support, breastfeeding education, referral hub
- **Postpartum care**: Emphasize that the 6-week visit is for **the parent's** health, not just the baby
- **Peer support**: Parent support groups, breastfeeding circles, postpartum wellness checks

## Critical Safety Rules
- Postpartum warning signs (teach BEFORE delivery): Severe headache, vision changes, swelling, heavy bleeding, fever, chest pain, difficulty breathing, thoughts of self-harm
- "If something doesn't feel right, trust your instincts and go to the ER. You know your body."
- Postpartum extends to 1 year — mental health and cardiac causes peak at 6-12 months post-delivery
