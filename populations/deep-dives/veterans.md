# Population Deep Dive: Veterans (VET)

> Operational guidance for professionals serving this community

---

## Health Profile
- **PTSD**: Affects 11-20% of veterans depending on service era; often co-occurs with depression and substance use disorders
- **Traumatic brain injury (TBI)**: Signature injury of post-9/11 conflicts. Linked to chronic headaches, cognitive impairment, and increased suicide risk
- **Substance use disorders**: Higher rates of alcohol and tobacco use than general population; opioid prescribing rates historically elevated in VA system
- **Suicide**: Veterans die by suicide at a rate 1.5x higher than non-veterans. Firearm access is the primary lethal means
- **Service-connected disability**: Musculoskeletal injuries, hearing loss, and toxic exposures (Agent Orange, burn pits) drive chronic illness burden

## Structural Drivers
- **VA access barriers**: Long wait times, geographic distance to VA facilities, complex eligibility rules that exclude many veterans
- **Military-to-civilian transition**: Loss of structure, identity, purpose, and social cohort. Benefits navigation is overwhelming
- **Toxic exposure legacy**: Decades of delay in recognizing burn pit, Agent Orange, and contaminated water exposures (Camp Lejeune)
- **Military sexual trauma (MST)**: Affects an estimated 1 in 4 women and 1 in 100 men who served. Underreported due to command structure
- **Discharge status**: Other-than-honorable discharges can bar access to VA healthcare, disproportionately affecting people of color

## Engagement Strategies
- **VA healthcare system**: Primary entry point. Community-based outpatient clinics expand geographic reach
- **Veterans Service Organizations**: VFW, American Legion, DAV provide peer community and benefits assistance
- **Peer support specialists**: Veteran-to-veteran connection is among the most effective engagement tools
- **Vet Centers**: Readjustment counseling centers offer confidential services outside the VA medical record
- **Community providers**: Many veterans use non-VA care. Train community providers in military culture competency
- **Stand Downs**: Annual events offering same-day services (dental, legal, housing, benefits) for veterans experiencing homelessness
- **Firearm safety conversations**: Means safety counseling framed as tactical, not political

## Screening Considerations
- **Military history screening**: Ask every patient, "Have you or a family member ever served in the military?" — many veterans do not self-identify
- **PTSD and TBI**: Use PC-PTSD-5 and brief TBI screens. Co-occurring conditions are the rule, not the exception
- **Toxic exposure history**: Screen for deployment locations and eras to identify burn pit, radiation, and chemical exposure eligibility under PACT Act
