# Population Deep Dive: Migrant and Seasonal Farmworkers (MSF)

> Operational guidance for professionals serving this community

---

## Health Profile
- **Pesticide exposure**: Acute poisoning and chronic low-level exposure linked to cancer, neurological damage, reproductive harm, and respiratory disease
- **Heat-related illness**: Farmworkers die from heat exposure at a rate 20x higher than the general workforce. Climate change is worsening this
- **Musculoskeletal injuries**: Repetitive motion, heavy lifting, and lack of ergonomic protections cause chronic pain and disability
- **Infectious disease**: Crowded housing, shared sanitation, and limited healthcare access increase risk of TB, COVID-19, and parasitic infections
- **Mental health**: Depression, anxiety, and substance use driven by isolation, family separation, exploitation, and fear of deportation

## Structural Drivers
- **Immigration status**: Fear of deportation prevents people from seeking care, reporting workplace injuries, or accessing public benefits
- **Labor law exclusions**: Farmworkers are excluded from many OSHA heat protections, overtime requirements, and collective bargaining rights that protect other workers
- **Employer dependence**: H-2A visa holders are tied to a single employer, creating power imbalances that suppress reporting of unsafe conditions or seeking medical care
- **Housing conditions**: Employer-provided housing is frequently overcrowded, poorly maintained, and located far from services
- **Mobility**: Seasonal migration disrupts continuity of care, insurance enrollment, and children's education and healthcare

## Engagement Strategies
- **Migrant health centers**: Federally funded health centers with specific expertise in farmworker health, including outreach during harvest seasons
- **Outreach workers (promotores)**: Bilingual, bicultural community health workers who go to the fields, camps, and housing to provide education and navigation
- **Farmworker legal aid organizations**: Legal Aid societies and organizations like Farmworker Justice provide know-your-rights education and enforcement support
- **Mobile clinics**: Bring care to where farmworkers live and work. Evening and weekend hours accommodate agricultural schedules
- **Mexican and Guatemalan consulates**: Trusted institutions that can connect nationals to health and legal resources
- **H-2A worker protections**: Ensure workers know their rights to medical care, safe housing, and transportation under H-2A contract terms
- **Pesticide safety education**: Provide in workers' primary language. Address right-to-know about chemicals used in the workplace

## Screening Considerations
- **Cholinesterase monitoring**: For workers exposed to organophosphate pesticides. Baseline testing before the season begins
- **TB screening**: High-prevalence population. Use IGRA or TST with appropriate follow-up for positive results
- **Depression and trauma screening**: Use culturally adapted tools in the worker's primary language. Screen for exploitation and trafficking indicators
