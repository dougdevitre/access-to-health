### 1.2 Change Management Communication Plan
When introducing significant changes (new EHR, reorganization, policy change, program sunset):

**Phase 1 — Pre-Announcement (2 weeks before)**
Audience: Leadership team only
Method: In-person meeting
Content: What's changing, why, timeline, expected questions, messaging alignment

**Phase 2 — Announcement (Day 1)**
Audience: All staff
Method: All-staff meeting (not email alone for major changes)
Content: What's changing, why, what stays the same, timeline, support available, who to ask
Template:
```
We're making a change to [specific thing]. Here's why: [honest reason].

What this means for you:
- [Impact on daily work]
- [Timeline]
- [Support available — training, help desk, transition period]

What is NOT changing:
- [Reassure about continuity]

I know change is hard. Here's how you can get help:
- [Contact person/resource]
- [FAQ document link]
- [Open office hours for questions]

Your feedback matters. Please share concerns with [person] or anonymously via [method].
```

**Phase 3 — Implementation (Weeks 1-4)**
- Weekly updates on progress and adjustments
- Dedicated Q&A channel (Slack, Teams, or office hours)
- Feedback loop: What's working? What's not?
- Adjust based on feedback (and communicate that you adjusted)

**Phase 4 — Stabilization (Month 2+)**
- Reduce update frequency to biweekly then monthly
- Celebrate milestones
- Document lessons learned

### 1.4 Staff Meeting Framework
**Monthly All-Staff Meeting (60 min)**

| Time | Item | Owner | Format |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0-5 | Welcome + good news | HDO | Verbal |
| 5-15 | Organizational update (budget, staffing, policy) | HDO | Presentation |
| 15-25 | Data spotlight (1 key trend or finding) | EPI/DAT | Chart + 2-min interpretation |
| 25-35 | Program spotlight (1 program shares a success and a challenge) | Rotating | Story + discussion |
| 35-45 | Cross-department issue or collaboration opportunity | Facilitator | Group discussion |
| 45-55 | Professional development micro-training (5-10 min skill share) | Rotating | Interactive |
| 55-60 | Recognition + upcoming dates | HDO | Verbal |

### 2.2 Board Presentation Rules
1. Maximum 8 slides for routine presentations. 12 for major decisions
2. One idea per slide. No walls of text
3. Data visualization > bullet points. Every chart needs a 1-sentence interpretation
4. Always state what you need from the board upfront (information only, approval, direction)
5. Anticipate top 3 questions and prepare backup slides
6. Practice to 10 minutes. Boards have many agenda items
7. End with: "What questions do you have?" not "Any questions?" (more inviting)
8. Follow up within 48 hours with meeting summary and action items

## Section 5: Frameworks and Standards Reference

### Reporting Frameworks Used in Public Health

| Framework | What It Covers | Who Uses It | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Healthy People 2030** | National health objectives (355 measurable objectives across 22 topic areas) | All levels, CHNA alignment | Decade goals, measured continuously |
| **PHAB Standards** | Health department accreditation (12 domains, 30+ standards) | Health departments seeking/maintaining accreditation | 5-year cycle + annual reports |
| **NACCHO Profile** | National Profile of Local Health Departments survey | All LHDs (voluntary but high participation) | Every 2-3 years |
| **ASTHO Profile** | State health agency profile | State health agencies | Every 2 years |
| **CHNA/CHIP** | Community Health Needs Assessment / Improvement Plan | Health departments + hospitals (IRS requirement for nonprofits) | Every 3 years |
| **Title V** | Maternal/child health block grant reporting (NOMs, NPMs, ESMs, SPMs) | State MCH programs | Annual |
| **PHEP** | Public Health Emergency Preparedness capabilities | Health departments receiving CDC PHEP funding | Annual |
| **CDC Cooperative Agreements** | Program-specific reporting per NOFO requirements | Grant recipients | Varies (annual + interim) |
| **BRFSS** | Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System | State-level (some local oversamples) | Annual |
| **County Health Rankings** | County-level health outcomes and health factors | All counties nationally | Annual release |
| **PLACES** | Census-tract level chronic disease, health behavior, and prevention estimates | All census tracts nationally | Annual |
| **Vital Statistics** | Births, deaths, fetal deaths, marriages, divorces | State/county registrars | Continuous, published annually |
| **National Notifiable Disease** | Reportable disease case counts | All jurisdictions | Weekly (MMWR) and annual |

### KPI Dashboard Design Standards
```
DASHBOARD DESIGN PRINCIPLES

1. HIERARCHY: Most important metric = biggest visual element (top-left)
2. CONTEXT: Never show a number alone. Show: Current value, target, trend, comparison
3. ACTIONABILITY: Every metric should answer "so what?" — what action does this data suggest?
4. EQUITY: Disaggregate by race/ethnicity by default, not as an afterthought
5. TIMELINESS: Display data refresh date. Flag stale data
6. ACCESSIBILITY: Colorblind-friendly, screen-reader compatible, plain language labels
7. AUDIENCE-SPECIFIC:
   - Board/executive: 6-8 KPIs, high-level, traffic light status
   - Program manager: 15-20 metrics, process and outcome, actionable
   - Community: 4-6 indicators, plain language, "what this means"
   - Funder: Tied to grant objectives, target vs. actual

STANDARD KPIs BY PROGRAM TYPE

Prevention Program:
- # screened, # positive, # referred, # connected, # with improved outcome

Clinical Service:
- # served, wait time, no-show rate, satisfaction, clinical outcome

Outreach/Education:
- # reached, # sessions, pre/post knowledge change, behavior change

Surveillance:
- Case count, incidence rate, timeliness of reporting, investigation completion rate

Emergency Preparedness:
- Exercise completion, plan currency, staff training %, activation response time
```
