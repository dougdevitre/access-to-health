## National Health Statistics

### Leading Causes of Death (US)
1. Heart disease: ~700,000 deaths/year
2. Cancer: ~600,000 deaths/year
3. COVID-19: Variable (check current year)
4. Accidents (unintentional injuries): ~225,000 deaths/year
5. Stroke: ~160,000 deaths/year
6. Chronic lower respiratory disease: ~150,000 deaths/year
7. Alzheimer's disease: ~120,000 deaths/year
8. Diabetes: ~100,000 deaths/year
9. Chronic liver disease: ~55,000 deaths/year
10. Kidney disease: ~55,000 deaths/year

### Health Disparities

**Life Expectancy**
- US average: ~77 years (verify current)
- White: ~78 years
- Black: ~75 years (gap narrowing but persistent)
- Hispanic: ~82 years (Hispanic paradox — despite lower income, longer life expectancy on average)
- Within metro areas: Up to 20-year gap between neighboring census tracts

**Maternal Mortality**
- US overall: ~32 per 100,000 live births (highest among wealthy nations)
- Black women: ~70 per 100,000 (3-4x white women)
- White women: ~19 per 100,000
- 80% of maternal deaths are preventable

**Infant Mortality**
- US overall: ~5.4 per 1,000 live births
- Black: ~10.9 per 1,000 (2x overall rate)
- White: ~4.4 per 1,000
- Preterm birth rate: ~10% overall, ~14% Black

**Chronic Disease**
- Diabetes prevalence: ~11% of adults (37 million)
- Hypertension: ~47% of adults (120 million)
- Obesity: ~42% of adults
- Cancer: ~1.9 million new cases/year

**Mental Health**
- 1 in 5 adults experiences mental illness annually (~57 million)
- Suicide: ~49,000 deaths/year (10th leading cause overall, 2nd for ages 10-14)
- Only 46% of adults with mental illness receive treatment
- 988 Lifeline: 24/7 crisis support

**Substance Use**
- Overdose deaths: ~110,000/year (fentanyl dominant)
- Alcohol use disorder: ~29 million adults
- Opioid use disorder: ~2.7 million adults
- Naloxone availability: Standing orders in most states

**Social Determinants**
- Food insecurity: ~44 million Americans
- Uninsured: ~27 million (8% of population)
- Homelessness: ~650,000 on a given night
- Poverty: ~12% of population (~38 million)

## Citation Rules

1. Always cite the source and year of any statistic
2. Use the most recent available data (note lag times — vital stats are typically 1-2 years behind)
3. For community presentations: Round to meaningful precision (say "about 1 in 5" not "19.7%")
4. Always provide context: "Higher than," "increasing since," "the gap has widened"
5. Disaggregate by race, ethnicity, income, and geography whenever possible (Guardrail #3)
6. Never present disparity data without naming structural causes
7. Never present disparity data without presenting your plan to address it
