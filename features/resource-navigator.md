# Resource Navigator — 9 Domains × 3 Tiers

> Match SDOH screening results to resources by domain and urgency tier
> Missouri reference implementation. Nationally applicable framework.

---

## How to Use This File

1. Run SDOH screening (`features/sdoh-screener.md`)
2. Identify flagged domains
3. Look up resources below by domain and tier
4. Make warm handoff referral (not just a phone number — call together, introduce, confirm appointment)
5. Document and follow up per protocol

**Tier Definitions:**
| Tier | Urgency | Timeline | Example |
|------|---------|----------|---------|
| **Tier 1: Emergency** | Immediate safety or survival need | Same day to 48 hours | Homeless tonight, no food, DV crisis |
| **Tier 2: Short-Term** | Basic need unmet, not immediately dangerous | 1-4 weeks | SNAP application, Medicaid enrollment |
| **Tier 3: Long-Term** | Stability and prevention | 1-12 months | Job training, permanent housing, education |

## Domain 1: Housing

### Tier 1 — Emergency
- **Emergency shelter**: Contact local 211 or Coordinated Entry
- **Domestic violence shelter**: National DV Hotline 1-800-799-7233 (24/7)
- **Hotel/motel voucher**: Through emergency assistance funds (Salvation Army, Catholic Charities, local emergency aid)
- **Warming/cooling centers**: During extreme weather events
- **MO-specific**: St. Patrick Center (St. Louis), Salvation Army Harbor Light, Community Council of St. Charles County

### Tier 2 — Short-Term
- **Rapid rehousing**: HUD-funded programs through local Continuum of Care
- **Transitional housing**: Typically 6-24 months with supportive services
- **Utility assistance**: LIHEAP, local utility company hardship programs
- **Rental assistance**: Emergency Rental Assistance Program (ERAP), local community action agencies
- **MO-specific**: Missouri Housing Development Commission, Beyond Housing (St. Louis County)

### Tier 3 — Long-Term
- **Housing Choice Voucher (Section 8)**: Through local public housing authority
- **Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC)**: Affordable housing developments
- **Permanent supportive housing**: For chronically homeless with disabilities
- **Homeownership programs**: HUD counseling, down payment assistance
- **Habitat for Humanity**: Homeownership through sweat equity
- **MO-specific**: Missouri Housing Trust Fund, MHDC First Place Loan Program

## Domain 2: Food

### Tier 1 — Emergency
- **Food pantries**: Feeding America network, local faith-based pantries
- **Emergency food boxes**: 3-7 day supply through local food bank
- **Soup kitchens / meal programs**: Daily hot meals
- **School meals**: Free/reduced lunch (even during breaks — Summer Food Service Program)
- **MO-specific**: St. Louis Area Foodbank, Harvester's (Kansas City), Ozarks Food Harvest (Springfield)

### Tier 2 — Short-Term
- **SNAP (food stamps)**: Application through DSS/FSD
- **WIC**: Women, Infants, and Children nutrition program — through local health department
- **Commodity Supplemental Food Program (CSFP)**: Monthly food box for seniors 60+
- **Meals on Wheels**: Home-delivered meals for homebound adults
- **Backpack programs**: Weekend food for school-age children

### Tier 3 — Long-Term
- **Double Up Food Bucks**: SNAP dollars matched at farmers markets
- **Community gardens**: Land, tools, seeds, education
- **Cooking classes**: Nutrition education for families
- **Produce prescription programs (FVRx)**: Doctor-prescribed fruits/vegetables
- **Food policy councils**: Systemic advocacy for food access
- **MO-specific**: Missouri Coalition for the Environment, Gateway Greening (St. Louis)

## Domain 3: Income / Employment

### Tier 1 — Emergency
- **Emergency financial assistance**: One-time payments for rent, utilities, prescriptions
- **Cash assistance (TANF)**: Temporary Assistance for Needy Families
- **General relief**: County-level emergency cash assistance
- **MO-specific**: Local Community Action Agencies, Salvation Army, St. Vincent de Paul

### Tier 2 — Short-Term
- **Unemployment insurance**: Through Missouri Department of Labor
- **Job training programs**: WIOA (Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act) funded
- **Job placement services**: Missouri Job Centers (formerly Career Centers)
- **EITC/CTC assistance**: Free tax preparation (VITA sites) to claim refundable credits
- **SSI/SSDI application assistance**: For people with disabilities

### Tier 3 — Long-Term
- **Career development**: Community college, trade schools, apprenticeships
- **Small business assistance**: SBA, SCORE, local business incubators
- **Financial coaching**: Budgeting, savings, credit repair, asset building
- **Individual Development Accounts (IDAs)**: Matched savings programs
- **MO-specific**: Missouri Job Centers, SLATE (St. Louis), Full Employment Council (Kansas City)

## Domain 4: Healthcare Access

### Tier 1 — Emergency
- **Emergency department**: For acute medical emergencies
- **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline**: Call or text 988 (24/7)
- **Crisis stabilization**: Mobile crisis teams, crisis receiving centers
- **SAMHSA Helpline**: 1-800-662-4357 (SUD referral)
- **Poison Control**: 1-800-222-1222

### Tier 2 — Short-Term
- **FQHC enrollment**: Federally Qualified Health Centers (sliding scale, no insurance required)
- **Medicaid application**: Through DSS/FSD or healthcare.gov
- **Marketplace enrollment**: ACA health insurance marketplace (open enrollment or special enrollment)
- **Prescription assistance**: Manufacturer patient assistance programs, NeedyMeds, RxAssist, 340B pharmacies
- **MO-specific**: Affinia Healthcare, Family Care Health Centers, Samuel Rodgers (Kansas City)

### Tier 3 — Long-Term
- **Primary care medical home**: Ongoing relationship with provider
- **Care coordination**: For complex/chronic conditions
- **Patient navigation**: System navigation for ongoing care needs
- **Health literacy education**: Understanding insurance, making appointments, self-management
- **Telehealth access**: For rural or transportation-limited populations

## Domain 5: Education

### Tier 1 — Emergency
- **School enrollment assistance**: For families in transition (McKinney-Vento Act)
- **Immediate childcare**: Emergency childcare assistance

### Tier 2 — Short-Term
- **GED preparation**: Adult education programs
- **ESL/English classes**: For limited English proficiency populations
- **Head Start / Early Head Start**: Early childhood education (ages 0-5)

### Tier 3 — Long-Term

- **Vocational training**: Trade schools, certificate programs
- **Continuing education**: Professional development, licensure support
- **Health literacy programs**: Understanding health information and navigating systems
- **MO-specific**: A+ Scholarship Program, Missouri Department of Higher Education

## Domain 6: Safety

### Tier 1 — Emergency
- **911**: Immediate danger
- **National DV Hotline**: 1-800-799-7233 (24/7)
- **National Sexual Assault Hotline**: 1-800-656-4673 (RAINN)
- **Child abuse hotline**: 1-800-392-3738 (Missouri), 1-800-422-4453 (national)
- **Safe shelter placement**: Through DV hotline or local shelter
- **Safety planning**: Immediate safety plan with trained advocate

### Tier 2 — Short-Term
- **Protective/restraining orders**: Through circuit court (legal aid assistance)
- **Legal aid**: Free legal services for DV survivors, housing, family law
- **Victim advocacy**: Court accompaniment, assistance with victim compensation
- **Counseling**: Trauma-specific therapy (EMDR, CPT, TF-CBT)
- **MO-specific**: Legal Services of Eastern Missouri, Crime Victim Advocacy Center

### Tier 3 — Long-Term
- **Support groups**: DV survivors, sexual assault survivors, trauma recovery
- **Permanent housing**: DV-specific transitional and permanent housing
- **Economic empowerment**: Job training and financial independence programs for survivors
- **Prevention education**: Healthy relationship education, bystander intervention

## Domain 7: Transportation

### Tier 1 — Emergency
- **Medical transport**: EMS for emergencies
- **Taxi/rideshare voucher**: Through health department or social services emergency funds
- **Volunteer driver programs**: Faith-based and nonprofit

### Tier 2 — Short-Term
- **Medicaid non-emergency medical transportation (NEMT)**: For Medicaid beneficiaries
- **Bus/transit passes**: Subsidized through social service agencies
- **Medical appointment transportation**: Through health plan or community health center
- **MO-specific**: OATS Transit (rural), Metro Transit (St. Louis), KCATA (Kansas City)

### Tier 3 — Long-Term
- **Vehicle repair/purchase assistance**: Programs like Ways to Work, Vehicles for Change
- **Driver's license assistance**: Including reinstated license programs
- **Transit advocacy**: Route expansion, frequency improvement, accessibility
- **Active transportation**: Bike share programs, sidewalk/trail advocacy

## Domain 8: Utilities

### Tier 1 — Emergency
- **LIHEAP**: Low-Income Home Energy Assistance Program (heating/cooling emergencies)
- **Utility company hardship programs**: Payment plans, disconnect protection
- **Cold/heat weather rules**: Utility disconnect moratoriums during extreme temperatures
- **MO-specific**: Missouri LIHEAP through local Community Action Agencies

### Tier 2 — Short-Term
- **Budget billing**: Level payment plans through utility companies
- **Arrearage management**: Debt forgiveness programs for past-due balances
- **Lifeline program**: Discounted phone/internet for low-income households
- **ACP (Affordable Connectivity Program)**: Internet subsidy

### Tier 3 — Long-Term
- **Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP)**: Home energy efficiency upgrades (free for qualifying households)
- **Solar programs**: Community solar, low-income solar installations
- **Energy efficiency education**: Reducing utility costs through behavior change
- **MO-specific**: Ameren Missouri ACT-ESS program, Missouri Weatherization

## Domain 9: Childcare

### Tier 1 — Emergency
- **Emergency childcare**: Through DSS or community crisis programs
- **Respite care**: Short-term relief for caregivers (especially special needs)
- **Crisis nurseries**: Temporary safe childcare during family crisis

### Tier 2 — Short-Term
- **Child care subsidy**: State-funded assistance for working families
- **Head Start / Early Head Start**: Free early childhood education (income-eligible)
- **Before/after school programs**: School-based and community-based
- **Summer programs**: Camps, enrichment, meal programs

### Tier 3 — Long-Term
- **Quality childcare placement**: Through Child Care Resource & Referral
- **Family childcare networks**: Licensed home-based providers
- **Employer-supported childcare**: Dependent care FSA, on-site childcare advocacy
- **MO-specific**: Missouri Child Care Aware, Child Care Resource & Referral network

## Referral Best Practices

### Warm Handoff Protocol
1. **Explain**: Tell the client what the resource does and why you're recommending it
2. **Ask permission**: "Would it be okay if I connected you with [resource]?"
3. **Call together**: Make the call with the client present (speakerphone or three-way)
4. **Introduce**: "This is [client first name], and I'm [your name] from [organization]. [Client] could use support with [domain]."
5. **Confirm**: Ensure the client has an appointment, address, and knows what to bring
6. **Document**: Record referral in client file — resource name, contact, date, appointment
7. **Follow up**: Per follow-up protocol in `features/sdoh-screener.md`

### What NOT to Do
- Do NOT hand someone a phone number and say "call this" (cold referral — low completion rate)
- Do NOT assume a resource is still available without checking (call first)
- Do NOT refer to a resource you haven't vetted (is it culturally appropriate? Accessible? Currently funded?)
- Do NOT make referrals without the client's informed consent
- Do NOT share screening results with the receiving organization without written consent

### Resource Maintenance
- Review all resources quarterly for accuracy (phone numbers, addresses, eligibility, hours)
- Maintain relationships with resource contacts (know a person, not just a number)
- Track referral completion rates by resource (if a resource never completes referrals, investigate)
- Add new resources as they become available
- Remove resources that are no longer operational
