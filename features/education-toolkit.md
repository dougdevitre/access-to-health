## Module 2: Community Health Education

### Program Design Framework (PRECEDE-PROCEED Model)

**Phase 1: Social Assessment** — What does the community want? (listening sessions, surveys)
**Phase 2: Epidemiological Assessment** — What are the health problems? (data analysis)
**Phase 3: Behavioral/Environmental Assessment** — What behaviors and conditions contribute?
**Phase 4: Educational/Ecological Assessment** — What predisposing, reinforcing, and enabling factors influence behavior?
**Phase 5: Administrative/Policy Assessment** — What resources and policies exist?
**Phase 6: Implementation** — Deliver the program
**Phase 7: Process Evaluation** — Did we do what we planned?
**Phase 8: Impact Evaluation** — Did behavior/environment change?
**Phase 9: Outcome Evaluation** — Did health status improve?

### Evidence-Based Health Education Programs

| Topic | Program | Evidence Level | Population |
|-------|---------|---------------|------------|
| Diabetes prevention | National DPP (CDC) | Strong | Adults with prediabetes |
| Chronic disease self-management | CDSMP (Stanford) | Strong | Adults with chronic conditions |
| Tobacco cessation | Quit for Life, Freedom from Smoking | Strong | Adult smokers |
| Falls prevention | A Matter of Balance, Tai Chi for Arthritis | Strong | Older adults |
| Healthy eating | Cooking Matters, Eat Smart Move More | Moderate | Families |
| Breastfeeding | Baby-Friendly Hospital Initiative | Strong | New mothers |
| Mental health first aid | Mental Health First Aid | Moderate | General public, youth |
| Sexual health | Making Proud Choices, Be Proud! Be Responsible! | Strong | Adolescents |

### Health Literacy Assessment
Before developing any public-facing material:
1. Assess audience literacy level (REALM, TOFHLA, or Newest Vital Sign)
2. Write at 6th-grade reading level (Flesch-Kincaid)
3. Use plain language principles (short sentences, active voice, common words)
4. Test with 5+ members of the target audience before finalizing
5. Produce in multiple formats (print, audio, video, pictorial)

## Module 3: Career Development in Public Health

### Public Health Career Pathway

```
Entry Level (0-2 years)
├── Community Health Worker (certification)
├── Health Educator (CHES certification)
├── Environmental Health Technician
├── Disease Intervention Specialist
└── Public Health Associate (CDC PMF/PHAP)

Mid-Career (3-7 years)
├── Epidemiologist (MPH + experience)
├── Public Health Nurse (BSN + PH experience)
├── Program Manager
├── Data Analyst (MPH or MS)
├── MCHES (Master CHES)
└── Licensed Professional Counselor

Senior/Leadership (8+ years)
├── Health Director (MPH/MPA + management experience)
├── State Epidemiologist
├── Division Director
├── Chief Health Strategist
└── Public Health Faculty
```

## Module 4: Public Health 101

### For New Staff, Board Members, Elected Officials, and Partners

**Session 1: What Is Public Health? (30 min)**
- Population health vs. clinical medicine (we treat communities, not just patients)
- The 10 Essential Public Health Services (updated 2020)
- Health department structure: local → state → federal
- Funding: Where does the money come from? (Federal grants, state funding, local taxes, fees)

**Session 2: Social Determinants of Health (30 min)**
- SDOH model: Conditions where people are born, grow, live, work, and age
- Zip code is a better predictor of health than genetic code
- The 9 SDOH domains (see `features/sdoh-screener.md`)
- Root causes: Structural racism, poverty, policy choices

**Session 3: Health Equity (30 min)**
- Equality vs. equity (equal resources vs. resources matched to need)
- Health disparities: What they are, who is affected, why they persist
- Structural determinants: Racism, sexism, classism, ableism as root causes
- What health departments can do: Data disaggregation, community engagement, policy advocacy

**Session 4: How the Health Department Works (30 min)**
- Our programs and services (overview of each division)
- Our data: What we track, how we use it, how to request it
- Our role in emergencies: Authority, partnerships, communication
- How to partner with us: MOUs, advisory boards, community engagement

**Session 5: Current Priorities (30 min)**
- Community Health Needs Assessment findings
- Community Health Improvement Plan priorities
- Strategic plan goals
- How to get involved

## Module 5: Train-the-Trainer (TTT)

### TTT Program Design

**Day 1: Foundations (6 hours)**
| Time | Topic | Method |
|------|-------|--------|
| 9:00-9:30 | Welcome, introductions, learning objectives | Icebreaker |
| 9:30-10:30 | Adult learning principles (Knowles) | Presentation + discussion |
| 10:30-10:45 | Break | |
| 10:45-12:00 | Facilitation skills: Questioning, managing discussion, handling difficult participants | Role play |
| 12:00-1:00 | Lunch | |
| 1:00-2:00 | Cultural competency in teaching: Adapting for language, literacy, culture | Case studies |
| 2:00-3:00 | Content mastery: Review of curriculum being trained | Study + quiz |
| 3:00-3:15 | Break | |
| 3:15-4:30 | Practice teaching: 10-min mini-lessons with peer feedback | Practice + feedback |
| 4:30-5:00 | Day 1 evaluation and prep for Day 2 | Reflection |

**Day 2: Practice and Certification (6 hours)**
| Time | Topic | Method |
|------|-------|--------|
| 9:00-9:30 | Review Day 1, questions | Discussion |
| 9:30-11:30 | Teach-back: Each participant teaches a 20-min section to the group | Practice + rubric scoring |
| 11:30-12:30 | Lunch | |
| 12:30-1:30 | Logistics: Scheduling, recruitment, materials, evaluation, reporting | Walkthrough |
| 1:30-2:30 | Fidelity: What you can adapt vs. what must stay the same | Discussion |
| 2:30-2:45 | Break | |
| 2:45-3:45 | Quality assurance: Observation, feedback loops, continuous improvement | Workshop |
| 3:45-4:30 | Action planning: Each trainer commits to dates, audiences, and goals | Individual planning |
| 4:30-5:00 | Certification, evaluation, and celebration | Certificates |

### Trainer Competency Rubric
| Competency | Developing (1) | Proficient (2) | Exemplary (3) |
|-----------|----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Content knowledge | Reads from materials | Explains concepts accurately | Answers questions beyond materials |
| Facilitation | Mostly lectures | Uses 2+ interactive methods | Adapts methods to audience in real time |
| Cultural responsiveness | One-size-fits-all | Acknowledges diversity | Adapts examples, language, and approach |
| Time management | Significantly over/under time | Within 5 min of target | Adjusts pace based on group needs |
| Engagement | Minimal audience participation | Most participants engaged | All participants actively involved |

**Minimum passing score**: 2 (Proficient) on all 5 competencies. Below 2 on any → additional practice + re-assessment.
